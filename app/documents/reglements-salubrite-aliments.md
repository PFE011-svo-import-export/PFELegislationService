# Part 1 — Interpretation

This Part sets out the definitions and interpretive rules that govern how terms are used throughout the *Safe Food for Canadians Regulations*.

## Definitions (Section 1)

The following definitions apply throughout these Regulations.

### Definitions: A to D

**Act** means the Safe Food for Canadians Act.

**carcass** means the body of a dead animal.

**catch-weight food** means a food that because of its nature cannot normally be portioned to a predetermined fixed quantity and is, as a result, usually sold in containers of varying quantities.

**close proximity**, in respect of an item of information shown on a label, means immediately adjacent to the item of information and without any intervening printed, written or graphic material.

**commercially sterile** has the same meaning as in section B.27.001 of the Food and Drug Regulations.

**common name**, in respect of a food, means: (a) the name printed in boldface type (not italics) in a section of the Standards of Identity Document; (b) the name printed in boldface type (not italics) in a section of the Food Compositional Standards Document; or (c) in any other case, the name by which it is generally known, or a non-generic name that describes the food.

**Compendium** means the document entitled *Canadian Grade Compendium*, prepared by the Agency and published on its website, as amended from time to time.

**consumer prepackaged**, in respect of a food, means packaged in a container in the manner in which the food is ordinarily sold to, used by, or purchased by an individual — or in which the food may reasonably be expected to be obtained by an individual — without being repackaged, to be used for non-commercial purposes.

**container** means an outer receptacle or covering used or to be used in connection with a food, including a wrapper and confining band, but not a conveyance or any container that is an integral part of a conveyance.

**contaminated**, in respect of a food, means that the food contains any micro-organism, chemical substance, extraneous material, or other substance or thing that may render the food injurious to human health or unsuitable for human consumption, including those not permitted under the Food and Drugs Act or that exceed any limits provided under that Act.

**dairy product** means milk or a food derived from milk, alone or combined with another food, containing no oil and no fat other than that of milk.

**Descriptive Words, Expressions and Identification Names Document** means the document prepared by the Agency and published on its website, as amended from time to time.

**dress** means to dress a carcass in accordance with section 145.

**drug** has the same meaning as in section 2 of the Food and Drugs Act.

### Definitions: E to L

**egg** means an egg of a domestic chicken of the species *Gallus domesticus* or, for processed egg products, that egg or an egg of a domestic turkey of the species *Meleagris gallopavo*. It does not include a balut.

**egg carton** means a package capable of being closed and containing not more than 30 eggs in separate compartments.

**eviscerate** means: (a) for the carcass of a bird other than an ostrich, rhea, or emu, to remove the respiratory, digestive, reproductive, and urinary systems (with or without the kidneys) and other thoracic/abdominal organs; or (b) for any other carcass, to remove the same systems except the kidneys.

**fish** includes shellfish, crustaceans, and other marine animals, and any of their parts, products, and by-products.

**food** has the same meaning as in section 2 of the Food and Drugs Act.

**food additive** has the same meaning as in subsection B.01.001(1) of the Food and Drug Regulations.

**food animal** means a bird or mammal, other than a marine mammal, from which an edible meat product may be derived.

**Food Compositional Standards Document** has the same meaning as in subsection B.01.001(1) of the Food and Drug Regulations.

**foreign state** includes a WTO Member as defined in subsection 2(1) of the World Trade Organization Agreement Implementation Act.

**Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Grades Document** means the document entitled *Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Grade Requirements*, prepared by the Fruit and Vegetable Dispute Resolution Corporation.

**game animal** means a wild ruminant, pig, or bird — including one living in an enclosed territory under conditions of freedom similar to wild animals — that is a food animal and is hunted for commercial use under an authorization issued by a competent authority.

**hermetically sealed package** means a package that, due to its design, is secure against the entry of micro-organisms, including spores.

**licence** means a licence issued under paragraph 20(1)(a) or (b) of the Act.

**livestock carcass** means a beef carcass, bison carcass, ovine carcass, or veal carcass.

### Definitions: M to P

**maple product** means a food obtained exclusively by the concentration of sap from trees of the genus *Acer* or the concentration of maple syrup.

**meat product** means the carcass of a food animal, the blood of a food animal, or a product or by-product of its carcass, or any food containing that blood, product, or by-product. It does not include: (a) gelatin, bone meal, collagen casing, hydrolyzed animal protein, monoglycerides, diglycerides, or fatty acids; or (b) food containing a meat product in an insignificant quantity.

**organic product** means a food commodity certified as organic under subsection 345(1) or certified as organic by an entity accredited by a foreign state referred to in subparagraph 357(1)(a)(ii).

**ornamental container** means a container that bears no advertising material (other than a trademark or common name), except on the bottom, and that is sold both as a decorative item and as a food container.

**poultry carcass** means the carcass of a turkey, duck, goose, guinea fowl, or bird of the species *Gallus domesticus*.

**prepackaged**, in respect of a food, means packaged in a container in the manner ordinarily sold to, used, or purchased by a person; includes consumer prepackaged.

**President** means the President of the Agency.

**principal display panel** — a detailed, container-type-dependent definition specifying which part of a label constitutes the principal display panel for various container configurations (display-card-mounted, ornamental, prepackaged, non-prepackaged, etc.). See subsection 1 for full text.

**processed egg product** means a food for which a standard is set out in Volume 2 of the Standards of Identity Document.

**processed fruit or vegetable product** means a fruit or vegetable in a hermetically sealed package that is commercially sterile, or that has been cooked, frozen, concentrated, pickled, or otherwise prepared for preservation, and that: (a) has a standard in Volume 4 of the Standards of Identity Document; (b) has a grade in Volume 3 of the Compendium; or (c) is identified as such in the Standard Container Sizes Document.

### Definitions: R to W

**ready-to-eat**, for an edible meat product, means it has been treated sufficiently to inactivate vegetative pathogenic micro-organisms or their toxins and control spores of food-borne pathogenic bacteria, so that no further preparation is required before consumption except washing, thawing, or warming without cooking.

**refrigerated**, for a food, means kept at 4°C or less without being frozen.

**sanitary condition** means a condition that does not present a risk of contamination of a food.

**shellfish** means a bivalve mollusc of the class Bivalvia or a carnivorous marine mollusc of the class Gastropoda, or any product derived from one of those molluscs.

**Standard Container Sizes Document** means the document entitled *Standard Container Sizes*, prepared by the Agency.

**Standards of Identity Document** means the document entitled *Canadian Standards of Identity*, prepared by the Agency.

**tray**, for eggs, means a package (other than an egg carton) capable of containing not more than 30 eggs in separate compartments.

**wine** means an alcoholic beverage complying with the standard set out in section 2.7.1 of Volume 2 of the Food Compositional Standards Document.

*Source: 2014, c. 20, s. 366(E); SOR/2022-144, s. 1; SOR/2024-244, s. 164; SOR/2025-192, s. 4.*

## Interpretation — Fresh Fruits or Vegetables (Section 2)

For the purposes of any provision of these Regulations that refers to "fresh fruits or vegetables," other than section 122, any fresh plant or any fresh edible fungus, or any part of such a plant or fungus, that is a food is considered to be a fresh fruit or vegetable.

**Exception — subparagraph 11(2)(c)(i):** For the purposes of any provision referring to "fresh fruits or vegetables," a food described in subparagraph 11(2)(c)(i) is not considered to be a fresh fruit or vegetable.

## Definition of "Prepare" in the Act (Section 3)

For the purposes of the definition *prepare* in section 2 of the Act, the growing and harvesting of any fresh fruits or vegetables are prescribed activities.

## Words and Expressions in Documents Incorporated by Reference (Section 4)

For the purposes of interpreting any document prepared by the Agency that is incorporated by reference into these Regulations, words and expressions that are used but not defined in that document have the same meaning as in these Regulations.

## Compendium Prevails (Section 4.1)

The requirements set out in the Compendium prevail over those set out in the Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Grades Document to the extent of any inconsistency or conflict between them.

*Source: SOR/2025-192, s. 5.*

# Part 2 — Trade

This Part sets out the requirements governing the interprovincial trade, import, and export of food commodities, including general safety standards, licensing-related prescriptions, and exceptions to those requirements.

## Subsection 10(1) of the Act — General Prescribed Commodity (Section 5(1))

For the purposes of subsection 10(1) of the Act, any food commodity is a prescribed food commodity that a person is prohibited from sending or conveying from one province to another — or importing or exporting — unless done in accordance with these Regulations.

## Subsection 10(2) of the Act — Import Licence Requirement (Section 5(2))

For the purposes of subsection 10(2) of the Act, any food — other than a food referred to in paragraphs 11(2)(a) to (c) — is a prescribed food commodity that a person is prohibited from importing unless authorized to do so by a licence.

## Subsection 10(3) of the Act — Compliance Requirement (Section 5(3))

For the purposes of subsection 10(3) of the Act, any food commodity is a prescribed food commodity that a person is prohibited from sending, conveying, importing, or exporting unless it meets the requirements of these Regulations.

## Section 12 of the Act — Possession for Trade or Export (Section 6)

For the purposes of section 12 of the Act, any food commodity is a prescribed food commodity that a person is prohibited from possessing for the purpose of sending or conveying from one province to another, or for export, unless it meets the requirements of these Regulations.

## Subsection 13(1) of the Act — Prescribed Activities for Trade Commodities (Section 7(1))

For the purposes of subsection 13(1) of the Act, any food commodity is a prescribed food commodity to be exported, sent, or conveyed interprovincially, and the following activities are prescribed activities that a person is prohibited from conducting except in accordance with these Regulations:

(a) manufacturing, preparing, storing, packaging, and labelling; and

(b) if the food commodity is an organic product, in addition to (a), advertising and conveying.

## Subsection 13(2) of the Act — Licensed Activities for Trade Commodities (Section 7(2))

For the purposes of subsection 13(2) of the Act, any food (other than one referred to in paragraph 11(2)(a) or (b)) and any food animal is a prescribed food commodity to be exported or sent/conveyed interprovincially, and the following activities require a licence:

**(a) In the case of a food:**
(i) manufacturing, processing, treating, preserving, grading, packaging, and labelling — with exceptions for: field packaging/labelling of fresh fruits or vegetables by the grower/harvester destined for further processing by a licence holder; packaging/labelling of certain Schedule 1 foods marked "For Further Preparation Only"; grading of livestock or poultry carcasses; and activities conducted by a restaurant or similar enterprise serving consumers directly; and
(ii) if the food is an edible meat product, also storing and handling in its imported condition for inspection purposes.

**(b)** In the case of a food animal: slaughtering.

*Source: SOR/2022-144, s. 2.*

## Interprovincial Trade, Import and Export — General Requirements (Section 8)

**Basic requirements (subsection 8(1)):** Any food sent, conveyed, imported, or exported must:
(a) not be contaminated;
(b) be edible;
(c) not consist, in whole or in part, of any filthy, putrid, disgusting, rotten, decomposed, or diseased animal or vegetable substance; and
(d) have been manufactured, prepared, stored, packaged, and labelled under sanitary conditions.

**Prohibition on mixing contaminated food (subsection 8(2)):** It is prohibited to mix a contaminated food with other food so that it meets the requirements of subsection (1), unless authorized by the Minister under subsection (3).

**Ministerial authorization (subsection 8(3)):** The Minister may authorize a person to mix a contaminated food with other food if of the opinion that no risk of injury to human health will result.

## Compliance with Standards (Section 9)

**Subsection 9(1):** Any food sent, conveyed, imported, or exported, for which a standard is set out in the Standards of Identity Document, must comply with that standard.

**Subsection 9(2) — Food likely to be mistaken:** Any such food that is likely to be mistaken for a food with a prescribed standard must also comply with that standard.

## Use of Food Additives and Other Substances (Section 10)

Any person who manufactures, processes, treats, or preserves a food that has been imported, or is to be exported or sent/conveyed interprovincially, may use a food additive or other substance in or on the food unless its use is not permitted by these Regulations or the Food and Drugs Act, or exceeds prescribed limits.

## Import — General Manufacturing Standard (Section 11)

**Subsection 11(1):** Any imported food must have been manufactured, prepared, stored, packaged, and labelled in a manner and under conditions providing at least the same level of protection as sections 47 to 81.

**Exceptions (subsection 11(2)):** Subsection (1) does not apply to:
(a) a food additive;
(b) a beverage containing more than 0.5% absolute ethyl alcohol by volume; or
(c) a food set out in Schedule 1 that is unprocessed, intended for use as a grain/oil/pulse/sugar/beverage, labelled "For Further Preparation Only," and is not consumer prepackaged.

## Import — Fixed Place of Business Requirement (Section 12)

**Subsection 12(1):** A person who imports a food (other than one in paragraphs 11(2)(a)–(c)) and lacks a fixed place of business in Canada must: (a) have a fixed place of business in a foreign state with a recognized inspection system (for meat products/live or raw shellfish) or an equivalent food safety system (for other foods); and (b) send the food directly to Canada from that foreign state.

**Minister's determination of food safety system equivalence (subsection 12(2)):** The Minister considers: (a) applicable legislative framework, controls, and procedures; (b) organizational structure of the responsible authority; (c) implementation; (d) supporting resources; and (e) any other relevant information.

**Exception — certain shellfish (subsection 12(3)):** "Shellfish" in paragraph (1)(a) excludes adductor muscles of scallops and geoduck meat.

**In transit (subsection 12(4)):** Food passing only in transit through a foreign state is not considered to have been sent directly to Canada from that state.

## Import Information Requirements (Section 13)

**Required information (subsection 13(1)):** Importers must provide the Minister with: (a) name, address, and licence number; (b) name/address of the sender; (c) foreign state of origin; (d) address of first Canadian destination; (e) description including common name and quantity; (f) any safety information required by the Minister; and (g) for live/raw shellfish (excluding scallop adductor muscle/geoduck meat), the establishment's registration or identification number.

**Timing (subsection 13(2)):** Information must be provided before or at the time of import.

**Exception for delayed reporting (subsection 13(3)):** For non-meat-product foods, the Minister may authorize, on written request, provision of information after import.

**Meat products clarification (subsection 13(4)):** Foods in paragraphs 25(a) and (b) are not considered meat products for subsection (3) purposes.

## Import — Further Inspection (Section 14)

**Subsection 14(1):** If further inspection is required at import: (a) for edible meat products, the licence holder must immediately deliver the product to an establishment for storage/handling pending inspection, and provide the inspector with the establishment's address if different from the declared destination; (b) for other foods, the importer must keep the food at the declared address until inspection is complete.

**Meat products clarification (subsection 14(2)):** Foods in paragraphs 25(a) and (b) are not considered meat products for this section.

## Interprovincial Trade and Export — Compliance Chain Requirements (Section 15)

**Subsection 15(1):** Food sent, conveyed, or exported interprovincially must meet requirements regarding: (a) licensed manufacturing/processing/labelling activities in Canada (with limited exceptions for field-packaged fresh produce, certain Schedule 1 foods, and carcass grading); (b) licensed importation; and (c) for meat products, licensed manufacturing, licensed grading, licensed importation, and licensed slaughter of source animals, as applicable.

**Exception (subsection 15(2)):** Subsection (1) does not apply to foods in paragraphs 11(2)(a)/(b), or to foods handled by a restaurant or similar enterprise serving consumers directly.

*Source: SOR/2022-144, s. 3.*

## Exception — Export of Non-Compliant Food (Section 16)

**Subsection 16(1):** A person may export food not meeting certain regulatory requirements (excluding paragraphs 8(1)(c)/(d) and subsection 15(1)) if labelled "Export," and either: (a) a document substantiates compliance with the importing foreign state's differing requirement; or (b) for specified requirements (subsection 9(1), sections 10, 195, 197, 201, 210, 244–249, 262, 268, 272, 273, 292, 293, 306–308, 312, 313, 316, 322, 324–327, 329, 331), a document sets out the foreign buyer's specifications.

**Document retention (subsection 16(2)):** Documents referenced above must be kept for two years after export.

**Additional meat product requirements (subsection 16(3)):** Exported meat products must also meet paragraphs 168(1)(a) and (b).

*Source: SOR/2022-144, s. 4.*

## Application for Export Certificates (Section 17)

**Application process (subsection 17(1)):** Applications for export certificates under section 48 of the Act must be made to the Minister in an approved form.

**Conditions for issuance (subsection 17(2)):** The Minister may issue a certificate if the applicant holds an export licence and the relevant food/commodity meets applicable Part 4 manufacturing/storage/labelling requirements.

**Additional prescribed food commodities (subsection 17(3)):** Animal- or plant-derived commodities not already covered by the Act's definition qualify as prescribed food commodities if a foreign state requires an export certificate for human-consumption import purposes.

**Exemption (subsection 17(4)):** Such commodities are exempted from provisions not necessary to give effect to this section, except section 6 of the Act.

**Pre-export inspection (subsections 17(5)–(6)):** The Minister may require inspection before issuing a certificate; the applicant must make the commodity accessible for inspection.

## Non-Compliant Food — Interprovincial Trade and Import (Section 18)

**Subsection 18(1):** A person may send, convey, or import non-compliant food (with specified exceptions relating to standard container sizes and grading for certain produce/honey) if: (a) labelled "For Further Preparation Only"; (b) brought into compliance within three months (or a longer Minister-approved period); and (c) for imports, the food is not a meat product.

**Exceptions (subsection 18(2)):** Paragraph (1)(a) does not apply to certain beef carcasses/cuts and processed fruit/vegetable products or honey labelled under section 306.

**Licence holder requirement (subsection 18(3)):** Activities under paragraph (1)(b), other than carcass grading, must be conducted by a licence holder.

*Source: SOR/2022-144, s. 5.*

## Exception — Import for Export (Section 19)

**Subsection 19(1):** A person may import non-compliant food (with specified exceptions) if: (a) labelled "Imported for Export"; and (b) intended for further processing before export.

**Licence holder requirement (subsection 19(2)):** Processing activities, other than carcass grading, must be conducted by a licence holder.

*Source: SOR/2022-144, s. 6.*

## Exception — Persons Who Convey Food (Section 20)

**Subsection 20(1):** The Act and Regulations do not apply to a person whose sole involvement with a food commodity is its conveyance.

**Exception (subsection 20(2)):** Sections 122, 123, and subsection 359(3) (and necessary supporting provisions) still apply to such persons.

## Personal Use Exception (Section 21)

For section 19 of the Act, sending, conveying, importing, or exporting food is considered solely for personal use if not intended for commercial use and either: (a) it is moved by an individual outside business activity, within quantity limits set in the Agency's published *Maximum Quantity Limits for Personal Use Exemption* document; or (b) it forms part of an immigrant's or emigrant's personal effects.

## Exception — Return to Canada of Exported Food (Section 22)

**Subsection 22(1):** Import requirements do not apply to food re-imported in its exported condition if: (a) for non-meat products, it is returned to the original exporter (if licensed) or the last person who handled it before export; or (b) for edible meat products, the import is inspector-authorized and the product is delivered to a licensed establishment pending inspection.

**Meat products clarification (subsection 22(2)):** Foods in paragraphs 25(a)/(b) are excluded from this meat product treatment.

## Exception — Interprovincial Trade, Import and Export — Special Categories (Section 23)

**Subsection 23(1):** The Act and Regulations do not apply to food that: (a) is carried for crew/passenger use on a conveyance; (b) is used for analysis, evaluation, research, or exhibition purposes, within weight/quantity limits; (c) is not intended as human food and is labelled accordingly; (d) is imported from the US into the Akwesasne Reserve for a permanent resident's use, or part of an in-bond shipment to a cruise/military ship; or (e) moves between federal penitentiaries.

**In transit (subsection 23(2)):** Food in an in-bond shipment passing only in transit through the US is not considered imported from the US.

*Source: SOR/2022-144, s. 7(F).*

## Exception — City of Lloydminster (Section 23.1)

**Subsection 23.1(1):** Prohibitions under sections 10–13 of the Act and these Regulations do not apply to food commodities moved between the Alberta and Saskatchewan portions of the city of Lloydminster.

**Definition (subsection 23.1(2)):** "City of Lloydminster" has the meaning assigned in the relevant Saskatchewan and Alberta provincial legislation.

*Source: SOR/2024-216, s. 1.*

## Exception — In Bond Shipment (Section 24)

The Act and Regulations do not apply to food that is part of an in-bond shipment sent between foreign states if: (a) the food is manufactured or prepared in a foreign state; and (b) it passes only in transit through Canada.

## Exception — Meat Products (Section 25)

Certain provisions (subparagraph 7(2)(a)(ii), subsection 28(2), paragraph 29(1)(d), subsection 31(2), section 42, paragraph 46(1)(b), and sections 69, 167, 168, and 296) do not apply to:

**(a)** Meat products (other than those in Table 2, Part A, Volume 7 of the Standards of Identity Document) that are mixtures of ready-to-eat meat product and non-meat food, provided the ready-to-eat component was domestically manufactured/graded/imported by a licence holder, or — if the mixture itself is imported — provided the exporting and source foreign states have recognized inspection systems and proper certification accompanies the import.

**(b)** Broth, lard, leaf lard, tallow, other rendered fat, suet, shortening, flavour, or extract derived from a meat product, under parallel conditions to those in paragraph (a).

# Part 3 — Licences

This Part governs the issuance, renewal, amendment, suspension, and cancellation of licences under the Act, as well as the inspection services associated with licensed food animal and meat product establishments.

## Division 1 — General

This Division sets out the general rules for licence applications, issuance, renewal, amendment, suspension, and cancellation.

### Paragraph 20(1)(a) of the Act — Import Licences (Section 26(1))

For the purpose of issuing an import licence under paragraph 20(1)(a) of the Act, any food is a prescribed food commodity.

### Paragraph 20(1)(a) of the Act — Export Licences (Section 26(2))

For the purpose of issuing an export licence under paragraph 20(1)(a) of the Act, prescribed food commodities are: (a) any food; (b) any food commodity referred to in paragraph 17(2)(b); and (c) any food commodity referred to in subsection 17(3).

### Paragraph 20(1)(b) of the Act — Import (Section 27(1))

For the purpose of issuing a licence under paragraph 20(1)(b) of the Act, any imported food is a prescribed food commodity, and storing/handling it in its imported condition (for inspector purposes) is a prescribed activity.

### Paragraph 20(1)(b) of the Act — Interprovincial Trade (Section 27(2))

For interprovincial trade licensing under paragraph 20(1)(b), prescribed food commodities are any food and any food animal, with prescribed activities being: (a) for food — manufacturing, processing, treating, preserving, grading, storing, packaging, and labelling; and (b) for food animals — slaughtering.

### Paragraph 20(1)(b) of the Act — Export (Section 27(3))

For export licensing under paragraph 20(1)(b), prescribed food commodities are any food, food animal, and commodities referred to in paragraph 17(2)(b) and subsection 17(3), with prescribed activities being: (a) manufacturing, processing, treating, preserving, grading, storing, packaging, and labelling (for foods/commodities); and (b) slaughtering (for food animals).

*Source: SOR/2022-144, s. 6.*

### Applications for Issuance, Renewal or Amendment (Section 28)

**General process (subsection 28(1)):** Applications must be made to the Minister in an approved form.

**Food animal/meat product applications (subsection 28(2)):** Applications for licences involving slaughter, meat product manufacturing/processing, or storage/handling of imported edible meat products must include at least one proposed work shift per establishment.

**Work shift definition (subsection 28(3)):** A work shift is: (a) for slaughtering — a period not exceeding 7.5 hours/day and 37.5 hours/week (excluding meals) during which an inspection station is operated; or (b) for other meat product activities — a period not exceeding the same hourly limits, or occurring between 6:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m.

### Conditions for Issuance, Renewal or Amendment (Section 29)

**Subsection 29(1):** The Minister may issue, renew, or amend a licence if: (a) the applicant is not in default of Agency fee payments (for issuance); (b) the applicant meets applicable Part 4 requirements; (c) for import licences, the applicant's fixed place of business is in Canada or a foreign state with a recognized inspection/safety system; (d) for slaughter/meat-product/storage licences, the President has approved a work shift per establishment; (e) submitted information is complete, truthful, and not misleading; and (f) the Minister believes the activity poses no risk of injury to human health.

**Renewal of suspended licence (subsection 29(2)):** The Minister may renew a suspended licence if requirements are otherwise met, but the suspension remains in effect.

### Refusal of Issuance, Renewal or Amendment (Section 30)

The Minister may refuse to issue, renew, or amend a licence if: (a) within the past five years, the applicant or a director/officer has had a licence suspended/cancelled or been convicted of an offence under the Act or Food and Drugs Act; or (b) for renewal/amendment, the applicant is in default of related fee payments.

### Establishment Requirements for Licence Holders (Section 31)

**Subsection 31(1):** A licence holder must conduct licensed activities (other than importing/exporting) in the establishment identified in the licence.

**Work shift requirement (subsection 31(2)):** Activities involving food animals or meat products must occur during an approved work shift with inspection services, unless otherwise authorized.

**Exception — ante-mortem examination (subsection 31(3)):** Ante-mortem examination may occur outside a work shift for slaughtering.

### Amendment of Licence — Inability to Conduct Activity (Section 32)

**Subsection 32(1):** If a licence holder cannot conduct a licensed activity at an identified establishment, the Minister may amend the licence to remove that authorization.

**Written notice (subsection 32(2)):** The Minister must notify the licence holder in writing of the amendment and its effective date.

### Expiry of Licences (Section 33)

**Subsection 33(1):** A licence expires two years after its issuance or renewal date, unless cancelled earlier.

**Expiry on amendment (subsection 33(2)):** Amending a licence does not change its expiry date.

### Invalidity of Licences (Section 34)

A licence becomes invalid if the holder surrenders it to the Minister and it is not subject to a cancellation procedure.

### Grounds for Suspension (Section 35)

The Minister may suspend a licence if: (a) the holder fails to comply with the Act (other than section 15), these Regulations, the Food and Drugs Act, or the Food and Drug Regulations; (b) the holder defaults on related fee payments; or (c) the Minister believes continuing the activity risks injury to human health.

### Suspension Procedure (Section 36)

**Subsection 36(1):** The Minister must not suspend a licence unless the holder: (a) received a written report setting out grounds for suspension and a corrective action period; and (b) failed to take corrective action within that period.

**Written notice (subsection 36(2)):** The Minister must notify the holder in writing of the suspension and its effective date.

### Immediate Suspension — Risk of Injury to Human Health (Section 37)

**Subsection 37(1):** Despite section 36, if the Minister believes continuing an activity risks injury to human health, the licence may be suspended immediately after providing a written report of grounds.

**Written notice (subsection 37(2)):** The Minister must notify the holder in writing that the suspension takes effect immediately.

### Duration of Suspension (Section 38)

A suspension must be lifted once the Minister determines corrective action has been taken.

### Grounds for Cancellation (Section 39)

The Minister may cancel a licence if: (a) corrective action is not taken within 90 days of suspension (unless extended); (b) the holder continues the activity while suspended; (c) the holder or a director/officer is convicted of an offence under the Act or Food and Drugs Act; (d) the holder has already been suspended for the same non-compliance, or suspended twice since issuance/renewal; or (e) the holder failed to comply with section 15 of the Act during application or the licence's validity period.

### Cancellation Procedure (Section 40)

**Subsection 40(1):** The Minister must not cancel a licence unless the holder was notified of grounds and given an opportunity to be heard.

**Written notice (subsection 40(2)):** The Minister must notify the holder in writing of the cancellation and its effective date.

## Division 2 — Inspection Services: Food Animals and Meat Products

This Division sets out how the Minister determines and adjusts inspection station numbers and minimum inspection hours for establishments handling food animals and meat products.

### Inspection Stations for Slaughtering (Section 41)

**Determining station numbers (subsection 41(1)):** The Minister determines the annual number of required inspection stations per work shift, per establishment, considering: (a) animal species slaughtered; (b) carcass examination/inspection method; (c) slaughter line speed; and (d) production volume.

**Fixed or unfixed locations (subsection 41(2)):** The Minister determines whether inspection services occur at fixed or unfixed locations, specifying fixed locations as needed.

**Additional stations (subsection 41(3)):** The Minister may authorize additional stations (annually or hourly) upon a licence holder's written request, if an inspector is available.

*Source: SOR/2022-144, s. 8(E).*

### Minimum Inspection Hours for Meat Products (Section 42)

The Minister determines minimum annual inspection hours per work shift, per establishment, considering: (a) nature/complexity of activities; (b) establishment size, equipment layout, and technology; (c) range of meat products and production volume; (d) work scheduling practices; and (e) inspection records for the establishment and comparable establishments.

### Inspection Services Outside Work Shifts (Section 43)

Inspection services may be provided outside a work shift if the licence holder submits a written request and an inspector is available.

### Notice of Changes Affecting Inspection Requirements (Section 44)

**Subsection 44(1):** A licence holder must notify the Minister in writing of changes affecting factors in subsection 41(1) or section 42, or if an annually authorized additional inspection station is no longer needed.

**Adjustment (subsection 44(2):** Upon learning of such a change, the Minister must reconsider and adjust inspection station numbers or minimum hours as appropriate.

# Part 4 — Preventive Controls

This Part sets out the preventive control requirements that operators and licence holders must implement to identify, prevent, and manage hazards throughout the food supply chain.

## Division 1 — Interpretation and Application

This Division defines key terms used throughout Part 4 and sets out the scope of its application.

### Definitions for Part 4 (Section 45)

**acceptable level** means a level of a biological, chemical, or physical hazard that does not present a risk of contamination of the food.

**agronomic input** means an input used in growing fresh fruits or vegetables, including agricultural chemicals, biological controls, pollinators, commercial fertilizers, compost, compost tea, green manure, manure, mulch, row covers, soil amendments, and pulp sludge.

**control measure** means a measure applied to prevent, eliminate, or reduce to an acceptable level any hazard presenting a contamination risk.

**critical control point** means a step where applying a control measure is essential to prevent, eliminate, or reduce a hazard to an acceptable level.

**low-acid food** means a food where any component has a pH greater than 4.6 and a water activity greater than 0.85.

**operator** means: (a) a licence holder for manufacturing/processing/storing/etc. a food, storing/handling imported edible meat products, or slaughtering food animals; (b) any person who grows or harvests fresh fruits or vegetables; and (c) any person who handles fish in a conveyance.

**scheduled process** means a process applying treatment to render food commercially sterile, accounting for critical physical/chemical factors affecting treatment effectiveness.

### Application of Part 4 (Section 46)

**General application (subsection 46(1)):** Requirements apply to: (a) foods to be exported or sent/conveyed interprovincially; (b) imported edible meat products during storage/handling for inspection purposes; and (c) food animals from which interprovincial/export meat products may be derived.

**Exception (subsection 46(2)):** Section 86 applies to import licence holders for any imported food, despite subsection (1).

**Application to establishments (subsection 46(3)):** Establishment-related requirements apply to: (a) the licensed establishment (for paragraph (a) operators); (b) the growing/harvesting establishment (for paragraph (b) operators); and (c) the conveyance (for paragraph (c) operators).

**Game animal slaughtering establishments (subsection 46(4)):** For specified sections, the facility within a game-animal-slaughter establishment is deemed to be the establishment.

**Exception — game animals (subsection 46(5)):** Sections 55, 56(1), 58, and 69 do not apply to game-animal-slaughter establishments.

## Division 2 — Biological, Chemical and Physical Hazards

This Division requires operators to identify, analyze, and control biological, chemical, and physical hazards that present a risk of food contamination.

### Identification and Analysis of Hazards (Section 47)

**Hazard identification (subsection 47(1)):** Operators must identify and analyze biological, chemical, and physical hazards presenting contamination risks.

**Prevention and control (subsection 47(2)):** Operators must prevent, eliminate, or reduce these hazards to an acceptable level using control measures with demonstrated effectiveness, including treatments/processes and, for meat products, measures in the Agency's published *Preventive Control Requirements for Biological Hazards in Meat Products* document.

**Imported food (subsection 47(3)):** Import licence holders must comply with subsections (1) and (2) for imported food.

## Division 3 — Treatments and Processes

This Division sets out requirements for treatments and processes applied to food, including the scheduled process for low-acid foods.

### Scheduled Process for Low-Acid Food (Section 48)

**Subsection 48(1):** For low-acid food in a hermetically sealed package, operators must apply the appropriate scheduled process and, if using batch thermal treatment, a temperature-sensitive indicator showing the package was thermally treated.

**Exception — refrigerated or frozen food (subsection 48(2)):** This does not apply if the food is kept refrigerated/frozen and labelled "Keep Refrigerated" or "Keep Frozen" on the principal display panel.

**Required documents (subsection 48(3)):** Operators must document: (a) for each low-acid food — the scheduled process description, responsible developer, and formulation; and (b) for each application — food name/volume, equipment used, treatment parameters, equipment maintenance/modifications, deviations and corrective actions, incubation results, and cooling water treatment.

**Retention period (subsection 48(4)):** Paragraph (3)(a) documents must be kept three years after the most recent application; paragraph (3)(b) documents must be kept three years after that application.

## Division 4 — Maintenance and Operation of Establishment

This Division sets out detailed requirements for how establishments must be maintained and operated, organized into subdivisions covering operator responsibility, sanitation, equipment, building conditions, storage, competency, and hygiene.

### Subdivision A — Responsibility of Operator

This Subdivision sets out the operator's overarching duty to maintain and operate an establishment in compliance with these Regulations.

#### Maintenance and Operation Duty (Section 49)

An operator must maintain and operate an establishment so that the requirements of sections 50 to 81 are met.

### Subdivision B — Sanitation, Pest Control and Non-Food Agents

This Subdivision sets out requirements for cleanliness, animal exclusion, and the safe handling of sanitizers and other non-food chemical agents.

#### Clean and Sanitary Condition (Section 50)

**Subsection 50(1):** An establishment and any conveyance/equipment used in a regulated activity must be clean and sanitary.

**Cleaning and sanitation manner (subsection 50(2)):** Cleaning and sanitation must be conducted without presenting a risk of contamination.

#### Animals in the Establishment (Section 51)

**Protection against animal entry (subsection 51(1)):** Establishments must be protected against entry of animals presenting a contamination risk, unless no reasonably practicable preventive measures exist for the relevant land.

**Animals in facility/conveyance (subsection 51(2)):** No animal may be present where food is manufactured/prepared/stored/etc. or food animals are slaughtered, unless it is: (a) a food intended for processing there; (b) a food animal intended for slaughter there; or (c) an animal intended for use in manufacturing/preparing food there.

**No contamination risk from measures (subsection 51(3)):** Measures taken under subsections (1)–(2) must not themselves present a contamination risk.

#### Sanitizers, Agronomic Inputs and Non-Food Chemical Agents (Section 52)

Any sanitizer, agronomic input, or non-food chemical agent in an establishment must: (a) be properly and clearly identified; (b) be suitable for its intended use without presenting a contamination risk; and (c) be handled/used per manufacturer instructions without presenting a contamination risk.

### Subdivision C — Conveyances and Equipment

This Subdivision sets out design, construction, and maintenance requirements for conveyances and equipment used in food handling and animal slaughter.

#### Conveyances and Equipment Used with Food (Section 53)

Any conveyance or equipment used in manufacturing, preparing, storing, packaging, or labelling food, or slaughtering food animals, must: (a) be appropriate for the food/animal and activity; (b) be designed/constructed/maintained to prevent contamination; (c) use materials suitable for their purpose and, if risk-presenting, corrosion-resistant, durable, capable of repeated cleaning/sanitizing, and free of noxious constituents; (d) have instruments to control/indicate/record necessary parameters; (e) function as intended; (f) be accessible and disassemblable as needed; (g) be used/maintained/calibrated per manufacturer instructions without contamination risk; and (h) have food-contact surfaces that are smooth, non-absorbent, and free from pitting/cracks/flakes (unless no contamination risk exists).

#### Other Conveyances and Equipment (Section 54)

Conveyances/equipment used for contaminated materials, waste, or inedible things must (unless no contact occurs): (a) be used only for that purpose; (b) be identified as reserved for that purpose; and (c) meet applicable requirements of section 53.

#### Equipment for Restraining Food Animals (Section 55)

Establishments where food animals are slaughtered must have restraining equipment for handling, assessment, ante-mortem examination, and inspection.

### Subdivision D — Conditions Respecting Establishments

This Subdivision sets out physical and environmental conditions that establishments must meet, including land, interior design, lighting, ventilation, temperature, water supply, and inspector accommodations.

#### Land and Location Risks (Section 56)

**Land (subsection 56(1)):** If establishment land presents a contamination risk, measures must be taken to eliminate it.

**Location (subsection 56(2)):** If an establishment is located near a contamination-risk place or thing, measures must be taken to eliminate the risk.

#### Interior Design and Construction (Section 57)

The interior of any facility/conveyance for food processing or slaughter must: (a) prevent accumulation of contaminants and permit effective maintenance/cleaning; (b) be designed/constructed/maintained with adequate size/layout, vermin prevention, smooth/non-absorbent/moisture-impervious surfaces (unless no risk), and good floor drainage (unless no liquid accumulation risk); (c) use suitable, appropriate, durable materials capable of repeated cleaning/sanitizing and free of noxious constituents; and (d) be of sound construction and good repair.

#### Separate Areas for Slaughtering Establishments (Section 58)

**Subsection 58(1):** Slaughtering establishments must have separate areas for: (a) keeping/examining/inspecting food animals; (b) segregating/isolating animals under sections 132/140(b); (c) holding animals showing behavioral/physiological/appearance deviations; and (d) humanely killing animals under paragraph 140(c).

**Inedible products area (subsection 58(2)):** An enclosed area for handling inedible meat products is also required.

**Movement safety (subsection 58(3)):** Floors, ramps, gangways, and chutes used by food animals must provide secure footing without injury risk during movement.

**Inspection stations (subsection 58(4)):** The establishment must have inspection stations at fixed locations/numbers set by the President, plus post-mortem examination or screening stations as applicable to authorized programs.

#### Design for Controlled Movement (Section 59)

**Subsection 59(1):** Facilities/conveyances must be designed/constructed/maintained to control movement of persons and things within, into, and out of them.

**No contamination risk (subsection 59(2)):** This movement must not present a contamination risk.

#### Separation of Incompatible Activities (Section 60)

Physical or other effective means must separate incompatible activities to prevent contamination.

#### Separation of Food (Section 61)

Physical or other effective means must separate food from: (a) anything presenting a contamination risk; (b) non-compliant food; and (c) anything manufactured/stored in the establishment not intended as food.

#### Arrival of Non-Compliant or Risk Food (Section 62)

**Identification on arrival (subsection 62(1)):** Food presenting a health risk, exempted under section 22, or non-compliant must be identified and placed in a designated area on arrival.

**Contamination prevention (subsection 62(2)):** Necessary measures must prevent such food from contaminating other establishment food.

#### Lighting (Section 63)

**Appropriate lighting (subsection 63(1)):** Establishments must have natural/artificial lighting appropriate for the food/animal and activity.

**Light fixtures (subsection 63(2)):** Fixtures must withstand repeated cleaning/sanitizing and not present contamination risk if broken.

#### Ventilation System (Section 64)

Facilities/conveyances must have ventilation systems that: (a) provide sufficient air exchange for clean air and odour/unclean-air removal; (b) are accessible and disassemblable as needed; (c) withstand repeated cleaning; and (d) function as intended.

#### Temperature and Humidity Control (Section 65)

**Appropriate levels (subsection 65(1)):** Temperature/humidity must be maintained at levels appropriate for the food/animal and activity.

**System requirements (subsection 65(2)):** Heating/cooling/humidity systems must, as necessary: (a) have control/indicating/recording instruments; (b) be accessible/disassemblable; (c) withstand repeated cleaning; and (d) function as intended.

#### Removal and Disposal of Contaminated Materials and Waste (Section 66)

**Means and systems (subsection 66(1)):** Establishments must have removal/disposal means and, if necessary, a functioning drainage/sewage/plumbing system.

**Frequency and manner (subsection 66(2)):** Removal/disposal must occur at a sufficient frequency and in a manner preventing contamination.

#### Cleaning Stations, Lavatories and Related Facilities (Section 67)

**Subsection 67(1):** If necessary to prevent contamination, establishments must have hand cleaning/sanitizing stations, lavatories, showers, drinking water stations, break rooms, or change rooms that are: (a) appropriately equipped and adequately sized/numbered; (b) readily accessible; and (c) capable of repeated cleaning/sanitizing.

**Hand stations (subsection 67(2)):** Hand cleaning/sanitizing stations must permit effective hand cleaning.

**Lavatories (subsection 67(3)):** Lavatories must be located/maintained without contamination risk.

#### Area for Inspector's Use (Section 68)

On request, inspectors must be provided a readily accessible, appropriately equipped, adequately sized area for exercising their powers and duties.

#### Office, Lockers, etc. for Inspectors — Meat/Egg Establishments (Section 69)

**Subsection 69(1):** Establishments processing meat products, processed egg products, or slaughtering food animals must provide inspectors with: (a) a furnished, accessible, appropriately equipped office; (b) accessible lockers/cabinets for equipment/documents; and (c) access to lockable storage for samples.

**Private facilities for slaughter establishments (subsection 69(2)):** Slaughtering establishments must provide a private office plus access to a lavatory, shower, and change room.

#### Water Quality — Contact with Food (Section 70)

**Potable water requirement (subsection 70(1)):** Water contacting food must be potable (unless no contamination risk) and protected against contamination.

**Steam and ice (subsection 70(2)):** Steam/ice contacting food must derive from water meeting subsection (1), unless no risk.

**Cross-connections (subsection 70(3)):** Potable water systems must not be cross-connected with other systems unless contamination risk is eliminated.

**Water for food animals (subsection 70(4)):** Water/hydration sources for food animals must not risk animal health injury or meat product contamination.

#### Supply of Water, Steam and Ice (Section 71)

**Subsection 71(1):** Establishments must be supplied with: (a) water adequate in quantity, temperature, pH, and pressure; (b) steam adequate in quantity/pressure; and (c) ice adequate in quantity — all appropriate for the food/animal and activity.

**Treatment manner (subsection 71(2)):** Any treatment of water/steam/ice must not present a contamination risk.

### Subdivision E — Unloading, Loading and Storing

This Subdivision sets out requirements for the conveyances and procedures used in unloading, loading, and storing food and food animals.

#### Conveyances Used for Unloading and Loading (Section 72)

Conveyances used to transport food to/from an establishment that are unloaded/loaded there must: (a) be designed/constructed/maintained to prevent contamination; (b) use durable materials capable of repeated cleaning/sanitizing and free of noxious constituents; (c) maintain appropriate temperature/humidity with control instruments as needed; (d) contain no risk-presenting animals, pest control products, or other contaminating materials; and (e) be clean and sanitary at unloading/loading time.

#### Manner of Unloading and Loading (Section 73)

Unloading/loading of food or food animals at an establishment must be conducted without presenting a contamination risk.

#### Storing of Food (Section 74)

**Food storage (subsection 74(1)):** Storing food must not present a contamination risk.

**Storage of other items (subsection 74(2)):** Storing conveyances, equipment, sanitizers, agronomic inputs, chemical agents, starter products, packaging materials, labels, or other processing-related items must not present a contamination risk.

**Definition of starter products (subsection 74(3)):** Materials used to start growing fresh fruits/vegetables, including seeds, seedlings, plants, cuttings, canes, seed potatoes, and nursery stock.

### Subdivision F — Competency

This Subdivision requires that persons involved in food handling or slaughtering activities have the necessary competencies and qualifications.

#### Competencies and Qualifications (Section 75)

Any person involved in manufacturing, preparing, storing, packaging, labelling food, or slaughtering food animals must have necessary competencies and qualifications.

### Subdivision G — Hygiene

This Subdivision sets out personal hygiene, clothing, and conduct requirements for persons working in food-processing or slaughtering areas.

#### Clothing, Footwear and Protective Coverings (Section 76)

Persons entering/present in food-processing or slaughtering areas must wear clean, sanitary, good-condition clothing, footwear, and protective coverings (gloves, hairnet, beard net, smock) appropriate for the food and activity.

#### Personal Cleanliness (Section 77)

Persons in such areas must maintain personal cleanliness, including hand cleaning/sanitizing: (a) immediately on entry; (b) immediately after using a lavatory; (c) immediately before starting the activity; and (d) at a frequency appropriate for the food/activity.

#### Spitting, Chewing Gum and Other Prohibited Acts (Section 78)

Persons in such areas must refrain from spitting, chewing gum, using tobacco, eating, unnecessary food contact, and other contamination-risk acts.

#### Objects and Substances Presenting Contamination Risk (Section 79)

Persons in such areas must refrain from wearing/using objects or substances presenting a contamination risk.

#### Reporting of Disease, Illness, Symptoms and Lesions (Section 80)

Workers in such areas with a disease, illness, symptoms, or open/infected lesion must report this to the operator.

#### Communicable Disease and Lesions — Operator's Duty (Section 81)

Operators must prevent persons suffering from or carrying a communicable disease, or with an open/infected lesion, from entering/remaining in processing/slaughtering areas if their condition presents a contamination risk.

## Division 5 — Investigation, Notification, Complaints and Recall

This Division sets out operators' obligations to investigate suspected risks, handle complaints, and carry out food recalls.

### Investigation of Suspected Risk (Section 82)

**Duty to investigate (subsection 82(1)):** Operators suspecting on reasonable grounds that a food presents a health risk or non-compliance must immediately investigate.

**Notification and mitigation (subsection 82(2)):** If the investigation confirms a health risk, the operator must immediately notify the Minister and take mitigating action.

### Complaints Procedure (Section 83)

**Written procedure required (subsection 83(1)):** Operators must prepare, keep, and maintain a documented complaints procedure.

**Handling complaints (subsection 83(2)):** Upon receiving a complaint, operators must implement the procedure, document the complaint/investigation/actions taken, and retain the document for two years after completion.

### Recall Procedure (Section 84)

**Written procedure required (subsection 84(1)):** Operators must maintain a recall procedure document naming responsible contact persons.

**Annual recall simulation (subsection 84(2)):** At least every 12 months, operators must conduct a recall simulation and document/retain its details for two years.

**Notice to Minister (subsection 84(3)):** Operators must immediately notify the Minister if a food should be recalled due to a health risk.

**Recall implementation (subsection 84(4)):** Operators must immediately implement the recall procedure and document/retain recall details (including effectiveness evidence) for two years.

### Imported Food — Investigation/Recall Compliance (Section 85)

Import licence holders must comply with sections 82 to 84 for imported food.

## Division 6 — Preventive Control Plan

This Division requires licence holders and certain growers/harvesters to prepare, implement, and maintain a written preventive control plan.

### Preventive Control Plan Requirement for Licence Holders (Section 86)

**General requirement (subsection 86(1)):** Licence holders must prepare, keep, and maintain a written preventive control plan meeting section 89 requirements for licensed activities.

**Exception — exported food (subsection 86(2)):** Not required for non-fish/non-meat exported food activities, unless an export certificate is sought.

**Exception — small sales (subsection 86(3)):** If gross food sales are $100,000 or less for the prior 12 months, a plan is required only for: (a) food animals, meat products, fish, dairy, eggs, processed egg products, or processed fruit/vegetable products in the licence; and (b) food for which an export certificate is sought.

### Preventive Control Plan Requirement for Growers/Harvesters (Section 87)

Growers/harvesters of fresh fruits or vegetables must maintain a plan if the produce: (a) is to be exported with a certificate sought; or (b) is to be sent/conveyed interprovincially and gross food sales exceed $100,000 for the prior 12 months.

### Implementation of Preventive Control Plan (Section 88)

Anyone required to maintain a preventive control plan must also implement it.

### Content of the Preventive Control Plan (Section 89)

**Subsection 89(1):** The plan must include: (a) measures ensuring compliance with specified labelling/grading sections; (b) measures ensuring packaging/labelling complies with subsection 6(1) of the Act; (c) for applicable hazard requirements — hazard descriptions, control measures and evidence of effectiveness, critical control points, critical limits, monitoring procedures, corrective action procedures, verification procedures, and supporting documents; and (d) for humane treatment requirements (sections 128–136, 140(b)/(c), 141–144) — parallel descriptions of measures, performance criteria, monitoring, corrective action, verification, and audit procedures.

**Document retention (subsection 89(2)):** Documents under subparagraphs (1)(c)(vii) and (d)(viii) must be kept two years after preparation.

**Exception — game animals (subsection 89(3)):** Game-animal slaughter licence holders need only include subparagraphs (1)(c)(i) and (d)(i).

**Additional content — import (subsection 89(4)):** Import licence holders must also address section 11 requirements.

**Additional content — export (subsection 89(5)):** Export licence holders must also address subsection 15(1) requirements.

**Additional content — post-mortem programs (subsection 89(6)):** Licence holders authorized under subsection 160(3) must also address their post-mortem examination or defect management program, per the Agency's published *Fundamentals* documents.

*Source: SOR/2022-144, s. 9(E).*

# Part 5 — Traceability

This Part sets out the documentation, production, and labelling requirements that enable food to be traced through the supply chain.

## Document-Keeping for Traceability (Section 90)

**General requirement (subsection 90(1)):** Persons sending/conveying/importing/exporting food, relevant licence holders, and growers/harvesters supplying food to others must document: (a) common name, lot code/unique identifier, and the responsible manufacturer's name/place of business; (b) (except retail sales) the date provided and recipient's name/address; (c) if received from another person, that person's name/address and date received; and (d) the name of any incorporated food commodity and its source details.

**Retail sale documents (subsection 90(2)):** Retail sellers (other than restaurants selling meals/snacks) must document the information in paragraphs (1)(a), (c), and (d).

**Retention period (subsection 90(3)):** Documents must be kept two years after provision/sale and be accessible in Canada.

## Production of Traceability Documents on Request (Section 91)

**Subsection 91(1):** Persons must provide requested documents to the Minister within 24 hours (or a shorter/longer Minister-specified period depending on health risk or recall necessity), and if provided electronically, as a single plain-text file usable by standard commercial software.

**Definition of plain text (subsection 91(2)):** Unencrypted data with available semantic content.

## Traceability Labelling Requirements (Section 92)

**General labelling duty (subsection 92(1)):** Persons under section 90 must ensure a label with the section 90(1)(a) information is applied to or accompanies food provided to another person.

**Consumer prepackaged food (subsection 92(2)):** For non-retail-packaged consumer prepackaged food, the unique identifier must be a lot code.

**Exceptions (subsection 92(3)):** Subsections (1)–(2) do not apply to food at export, non-consumer-prepackaged food at retail sale, or certain prepackaged foods at retail sale.

**Further exceptions (subsections 92(4)–(5)):** Common name labelling is not required for certain foods at retail sale; name/place-of-business labelling is not required for foods described in section 220.

# Part 6 — Commodity-specific Requirements

This Part sets out requirements specific to particular food commodities, including dairy products, eggs, fish, fresh fruits and vegetables, and meat products and food animals.

## Division 1 — Application

This Division sets out the scope of application of the commodity-specific requirements in Part 6.

### Scope of Part 6 (Section 93)

**General application (subsection 93(1)):** Requirements apply to: (a) imported, interprovincially-traded, or exported foods; and (b) food animals from which interprovincial/export meat products may derive.

**Food animal application (subsection 93(2)):** Sections 128–136 apply to food animals in a licensed slaughter establishment.

## Division 2 — Dairy Products

This Division sets out preparation standards applicable to dairy products intended for interprovincial trade or export.

### Preparation Standards for Dairy Products (Section 94)

Milk or cream used in preparing interprovincially-traded or exported dairy products must meet the applicable provincial legislation requirements of the province where prepared.

## Division 3 — Eggs

This Division sets out requirements for the pasteurization, importation, grading, interprovincial trade, and handling of eggs.

### Pasteurization Requirements (Section 95)

**Grade requirement (subsection 95(1)):** Licence holders may pasteurize eggs in the shell only if graded Canada A or Grade A.

**Imported eggs (subsection 95(2)):** Imported eggs pasteurized in the shell must have been graded Grade A before pasteurization.

### Import — Foreign Official Document Required (Section 96)

Import licence holders may import eggs only with an official foreign document (in an approved form) confirming compliance with the Act and Regulations.

### Import of Grade C or Nest Run Eggs (Section 97)

Licence holders importing Grade C or Grade Nest Run eggs must deliver them directly to an establishment where eggs are processed/treated by a licence holder.

### Import of Ungraded Eggs (Section 98)

**Conditions (subsection 98(1)):** Licence holders may import ungraded eggs if they: (a) notify the Minister in writing beforehand of quantity, date, and destination establishment; (b) label the container "Ungraded Eggs"; and (c) deliver directly to a processing/treating establishment.

**Removal from establishment (subsection 98(2)):** Imported ungraded eggs may be removed once processed/treated, or if delivered directly to another processing establishment.

### Interprovincial Trade of Specific Egg Categories (Section 99)

**Delivery to processing establishment (subsection 99(1)):** Persons sending interprovincially dyed/ink-marked Canada A/B eggs, Canada C eggs, imported Grade C/Nest Run eggs, or imported ungraded eggs (under section 98) must deliver them to a licensed processing/treating establishment.

**Canada Nest Run (subsection 99(2)):** Must be delivered to a grading or processing/treating establishment.

**Ungraded eggs generally (subsection 99(3)):** May be sent interprovincially (excluding rejected or section-98 imported eggs) if labelled "Ungraded Eggs" and delivered to a grading or processing/treating establishment.

### Ink Used on Egg Shells (Section 100)

Ink applied to egg shells by a licence holder must be fast-drying, indelible, and not present a risk of injury to human health.

### Trays for Egg Producers (Section 101)

Before sending plastic trays to an egg producer, licence holders must clean, sanitize, and dry them.

## Division 4 — Processed Egg Products

This Division sets out requirements for the processing, treatment, temperature control, and importation of processed egg products.

### Processing and Treating Standards for Eggs (Section 102)

**Eggs eligible for processing (subsection 102(1)):** Licence holders may process/treat eggs only if they are edible, odor-free, not moldy, never incubated, free of significant internal defects, not leakers (with limited exception), and free of dirt/foreign matter.

**Processed egg products (subsection 102(2)):** May only be processed/treated if derived from eggs meeting subsection (1) requirements.

### Temperature Requirements for Processed Egg Products (Section 103)

**Chilling requirement (subsection 103(1)):** Liquid whole egg, yolk, white/albumen, whole egg mix, yolk mix, and liquid egg product destined for interprovincial trade or export must be chilled to 4°C or less before removal from the establishment.

**Ministerial exception (subsection 103(2)):** The Minister may authorize removal without chilling if no health risk results.

### Import — Foreign Official Document for Processed Egg Products (Section 104)

Import licence holders may import processed egg products only with an official foreign document confirming compliance.

## Division 5 — Fish

This Division sets out prohibitions and requirements concerning the importation and handling of fish and shellfish.

### Prohibition on Importing Mitten Crab or Puffer Fish (Section 105)

**Subsection 105(1):** It is prohibited to import: (a) live freshwater mitten crab (genus *Eriocheir*); or (b) puffer fish (family Tetraodontidae).

**Personal use exception inapplicable (subsection 105(2)):** Section 19 of the Act (personal use exception) does not apply to this prohibition.

### Import of Live or Raw Shellfish (Section 106)

**Conditions (subsection 106(1)):** Import licence holders may import live/raw shellfish only if: (a) the harvesting/processing foreign state has a recognized inspection system; (b) the exporting foreign state has a recognized inspection system; and (c) the processing establishment complies with a recognized system.

**Exception (subsection 106(2)):** These conditions do not apply to scallop adductor muscles or geoduck meat.

### Shellfish Harvest Area Requirements (Section 107)

**Subsection 107(1):** Licence holders may process shellfish for interprovincial trade/export only if harvested in an area: (a) classified under the Canadian Shellfish Sanitation Program and not subject to a fishing prohibition order; or (b) subject to such an order but decontaminated per an approved plan.

**Exception (subsection 107(2)):** Does not apply to scallop adductor muscles or geoduck meat.

### Frozen Fish Storage Protection (Section 108)

Licence holders must protect frozen fish stored in a conveyance from dehydration and oxidation.

## Division 6 — Fresh Fruits or Vegetables

This Division sets out interpretation, import, and trade requirements specific to fresh fruits and vegetables, organized into three subdivisions.

### Subdivision A — Interpretation and Application

This Subdivision defines terms specific to fresh fruits or vegetables and sets out packaging exceptions for mixed-produce containers.

#### Definitions for Fresh Fruits or Vegetables (Section 109)

**apple** means a fresh apple for which a grade is prescribed by these Regulations.

**onion** means a fresh onion for which a grade is prescribed by these Regulations.

**potato** means a fresh potato for which a grade is prescribed by these Regulations.

#### Fresh Fruits or Vegetables Packaged Together (Section 110)

Certain requirements (sections 113–121, 269, and applicable Division 2 of Part 10/Part 12 provisions) do not apply to consumer prepackaged mixed fresh fruits/vegetables (no other food) if: (a) labelled "Fresh Pack" or, for vegetables, "Stew-pack"/"Vegetables for Stew"; (b) no single type exceeds 1 kg net weight; and (c) total net weight does not exceed 10 kg.

#### Fresh Fruits or Vegetables Packaged with Other Food (Section 111)

The same exception applies to consumer prepackaged fresh fruits/vegetables combined with other food if: (a) labelled "Gift Pack" or "Combo Pack"; (b) no single type exceeds 1 kg net weight; and (c) total net weight does not exceed 10 kg.

### Subdivision B — Import

This Subdivision sets out grade, certification, and documentation requirements for imported fresh fruits and vegetables, including onions, potatoes, and apples.

#### Whole Fresh Fruits or Vegetables — Scope (Section 112)

This Subdivision's requirements apply to whole fresh fruits or vegetables.

#### Imported Potatoes — Grade Requirements (Section 113)

**Standard (subsection 113(1)):** Imported potatoes must meet Canada No. 1 grade requirements in the Compendium or Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Grades Document.

**US presumption (subsection 113(2)):** US-imported potatoes are presumed compliant if graded in the US per the Agency's published US import grade requirements document.

*Source: SOR/2025-192, s. 6.*

#### Imported Apples — Grade Requirements (Section 114)

**Non-US foreign states (subsection 114(1)):** Must meet Canada Extra Fancy, Canada Fancy, or Canada Commercial grade requirements.

**US imports (subsection 114(2)):** Must meet Canada Extra Fancy or Canada Fancy grade requirements.

**US presumption (subsection 114(3)):** US-graded apples are presumed compliant per the Agency's published US import grade requirements document.

*Source: SOR/2025-192, s. 6.*

#### General Presumption for US-Imported Produce (Section 115)

Fresh fruits/vegetables (other than potatoes/apples) imported from the US are presumed compliant with Compendium/Grades Document requirements if graded in the US per the Agency's published US import grade requirements document.

*Source: SOR/2025-192, s. 6.*

#### Foreign States — Onions, Potatoes and Apples Certification (Section 116)

Onions/potatoes from non-US foreign states, and apples from foreign states other than the US/New Zealand, must meet and be Minister-certified as meeting: (a) applicable Parts 10–12 requirements; (b) for onions, the relevant grade requirements; (c) for potatoes, Canada No. 1 grade; and (d) for apples, Canada Extra Fancy/Fancy/Commercial grade.

*Source: SOR/2025-192, s. 7.*

#### Onions, Potatoes and Apples from the United States (Section 117)

**Subsection 117(1):** Must either: (a) be accompanied at the Canadian port of entry by a serially numbered certificate (or facsimile/email copy) from the US federal agriculture department confirming compliance with Parts 10–12 and applicable grade standards; or (b) meet the Parts 10–12 requirements and be Minister-certified as meeting applicable grade standards (subject to general inspection tolerances).

**Endorsement requirement (subsection 117(2)):** The certificate/evidence must bear the expression "Meets Canadian Import Requirements for Grades, Packaging, Labelling and Standard Container Size."

*Source: SOR/2025-192, s. 8.*

#### Apples from New Zealand (Section 118)

**Subsection 118(1):** Must either: (a) be accompanied by a serially numbered New Zealand ministry certificate confirming compliance with Parts 10–12 and grade standards; or (b) meet Parts 10–12 requirements and be Minister-certified as meeting grade standards.

**Endorsement requirement (subsection 118(2)):** Same endorsement language as section 117.

*Source: SOR/2025-192, s. 9.*

#### Exception for Small Shipments (Section 119)

Sections 116–118 do not apply to onions, potatoes, or apples in shipments of: (a) at most 15 containers/250 kg of onions; (b) at most 15 containers/250 kg of potatoes; and (c) at most 15 containers/250 kg of apples.

#### In-Transit Shipments Through the United States (Section 120)

For sections 113–119, produce shipped to Canada in bond from a non-US foreign state, passing only in transit through the US, is not considered imported from the US.

#### Application for Grade Certificate (Section 121)

**Application process (subsection 121(1)):** Applications under section 116 or paragraphs 117(1)(b)/118(1)(b) must be made to the Minister in an approved form.

**Inspection (subsections 121(2)–(3)):** The Minister may require inspection; applicants must make the produce accessible.

### Subdivision C — Trade of Fresh Fruits or Vegetables

This Subdivision sets out registration requirements for trading fresh fruits or vegetables and procedures for reporting damaged or defective produce.

#### Prohibition on Trading Without Registration (Section 122)

**Subsection 122(1):** It is prohibited to: (a) sell fresh fruits/vegetables destined for export/interprovincial trade; (b) purchase/negotiate purchase on another's behalf for import/interprovincial trade; (c) receive imported/interprovincially-traded produce; or (d) send/convey/import/export such produce.

**Exceptions (subsection 122(2)):** Does not apply to: (a) Fruit and Vegetable Dispute Resolution Corporation members in good standing; (b) small direct-to-consumer sellers (under $100,000/year); (c) small-volume traders (under 1 metric ton/day); (d) growers selling only their own produce; or (e) registered charities and certain tax-exempt clubs/associations.

**Exception — nuts and wild produce (subsection 122(3)):** Does not apply to nuts, wild fruit, and wild vegetables.

#### Damaged or Defective Produce — Reports and Notices (Section 123)

**Request for report/notice (subsection 123(1)):** Persons with a financial interest in damaged/defective interprovincial or imported produce may request: (a) a written condition report; or (b) a written disposition notice.

**Application process (subsection 123(2)):** Requests must be made to the Minister in an approved form.

**Accessibility for inspection (subsection 123(3)):** Produce must be made accessible for inspection.

**Witnessed disposition (subsection 123(4)):** Disposition must be witnessed by an inspector.

## Division 7 — Meat Products and Food Animals

This Division sets out requirements specific to meat products and food animals, covering edibility determinations, humane treatment, ante-mortem and post-mortem procedures, and slaughtering and dressing standards.

### Subdivision A — Interpretation

This Subdivision defines terms used throughout the Division concerning meat products and food animals.

#### Definitions for Division 7 (Section 124)

**on-farm food safety program** means a program operated on a farm or similar place under which hazards relating to meat product safety from food animals are identified, analyzed, and controlled.

**specified risk material** has the same meaning as in section 6.1 of the Health of Animals Regulations.

**split** means to cut a carcass lengthwise along the median line.

**veterinary inspector** means a person designated as such under subsection 13(3) of the Canadian Food Inspection Agency Act.

### Subdivision B — Edible Meat Products

This Subdivision sets out the conditions under which a meat product may be identified as edible, and the requirements for natural casings.

#### Identification of Meat Products as Edible (Section 125)

**Conditions for edible designation (subsection 125(1)):** A licence holder may identify a meat product as edible only if: (a) the source food animal (or shipment sample) underwent ante-mortem examination; (b) (except game animals) it underwent ante-mortem inspection; (c) the carcass was dressed/partially dressed; (d) the carcass, parts, and blood underwent post-mortem inspection or examination; and (e) the product is edible, uncontaminated, and free of specified risk material.

**Additional requirements for specific products (subsection 125(2)):** Licence holders must not identify as edible: (a) a heart (with limited exceptions) unless opened/inverted with blood clots/vessels removed; (b) a liver unless the gallbladder is removed; (c) a gizzard unless contents/lining are removed and washed; (d) a meat product containing a urinary bladder/intestine unless used as compliant natural casing; or (e) a meat product with artificial casing unless made from noxious-constituent-free material.

#### Natural Casings (Section 126)

A urinary bladder, intestine, or part thereof may serve as natural casing if: (a) emptied, washed, and mucous lining removed; (b) for a bladder, inverted, brined at least 12 hours, then rinsed; and (c) the casing is clean.

### Subdivision C — Humane Treatment

This Subdivision sets out requirements for the humane handling, treatment, and monitoring of food animals to prevent avoidable suffering, injury, or death.

#### Clarification — "Avoidable Death" (Section 127)

For greater certainty, "avoidable death" in these Regulations does not include slaughter conducted in accordance with these Regulations or humane killing.

#### General Prohibition on Avoidable Suffering, Injury or Death (Section 128)

Licence holders must handle food animals at the establishment without causing avoidable suffering, injury, or death, and must not expose them to conditions that may cause such harm.

#### Prohibition on Hitting Food Animals (Section 129)

**General prohibition (subsection 129(1)):** Licence holders must not hit a food animal with a whip, prod, or (except under section 141) any other object.

**Electric prod conditions (subsection 129(2)):** An electric prod may be applied only if: (a) no reasonably practicable movement alternative exists; (b) the animal is a pig or bovine; (c) it is applied to the lateral rear leg muscles between hock and hip joints; (d) the animal has sufficient forward space; (e) its movement ability is not compromised; and (f) it does not cause avoidable suffering, injury, or death.

#### Assessing and Monitoring Food Animal Condition (Section 130)

**Initial assessment (subsection 130(1)):** Licence holders must assess for signs of suffering/injury on arrival.

**Ongoing monitoring (subsection 130(2)):** Animals must be monitored regularly thereafter, including conditions that may cause avoidable harm.

**Corrective action (subsection 130(3)):** If harmful conditions are identified, immediate corrective action is required.

**Immediate measures for suffering (subsection 130(4)):** If an animal shows suffering signs, the licence holder must immediately: (a) alleviate suffering; (b) humanely kill it; or (c) slaughter it per these Regulations.

**Exception — game animals (subsection 130(5)):** This section does not apply to game animals.

#### Game Animals — Monitoring and Corrective Action (Section 131)

Licence holders with direct control over game animals must: (a) monitor regularly for harmful conditions; (b) immediately correct such conditions; and (c) if showing suffering signs, immediately alleviate suffering, humanely kill, or slaughter per these Regulations.

#### Segregation and Isolation of Food Animals (Section 132)

Licence holders must: (a) segregate different species; (b) segregate or isolate sick/injured animals as their condition requires; and (c) isolate animals that may cause harm to others due to nature, temperament, gender, weight, age, or other cause.

#### Prevention of Overcrowding (Section 133)

Licence holders must provide sufficient space to prevent suffering, injury, or death.

#### Adequate Ventilation for Food Animals (Section 134)

Licence holders must provide sufficient ventilation to prevent suffering, injury, or death.

#### Safe Handling of Food Animals (Section 135)

**Competency requirement (subsection 135(1)):** Anyone handling a food animal (including in a crate) must: (a) be competently qualified to do so without causing avoidable harm; and (b) use equipment in a manner/circumstance that avoids avoidable harm.

**Establishment areas and equipment (subsection 135(2)):** Only areas/equipment designed, constructed, and maintained to avoid avoidable harm may be used for handling.

#### Water and Feed Requirements (Section 136)

**Unloaded animals (subsection 136(1)):** Licence holders must provide unloaded food animals (other than those confined in crates) with: (a) water/hydration immediately upon unloading; and (b) feed within 24 hours.

**Crated animals (subsection 136(2)):** Crated animals must receive water/hydration and feed within 24 hours of arrival.

### Subdivision D — Removal and Keeping

This Subdivision sets out notification requirements for removing food animals from an establishment or keeping them there for an extended period.

#### Notification Requirements for Removal and Extended Keeping (Section 137)

**Before removal (subsection 137(1)):** Licence holders must notify a veterinary inspector before removing a food animal from an establishment.

**Extended keeping (subsection 137(2)):** Notification is also required before keeping an animal more than seven days.

### Subdivision E — Ante-mortem Examination and Inspection

This Subdivision sets out the procedures for ante-mortem examination and inspection of food animals before slaughter, including the handling of condemned animals.

#### Ante-mortem Examination Procedure (Section 138)

**Timing and procedure (subsection 138(1)):** Within 24 hours before slaughter, licence holders must conduct an ante-mortem examination per the Agency's published procedures document, including document review for equines/birds (excluding game animals, ostriches, rheas, emus).

**Handling deviations (subsection 138(2)):** If a deviation from normal behavior/physiology/appearance is suspected, the animal must be held for veterinary inspection unless otherwise authorized.

#### Ante-mortem Inspection Procedure (Section 139)

**Presentation requirement (subsection 139(1)):** Within 24 hours before slaughter (excluding game animals), licence holders must present the animal (or shipment sample) and relevant documents to a veterinary inspector or supervised inspector.

**Handling deviations (subsection 139(2)):** If a non-veterinary inspector suspects a deviation, the animal must be held for veterinary inspection.

*Source: SOR/2022-144, s. 10(E).*

#### Condemnation Procedure (Section 140)

If a veterinary inspector (or supervised inspector) condemns a food animal after determining its meat product could not be identified as edible, the licence holder must: (a) identify the animal as inedible; (b) segregate/isolate it as needed; (c) humanely kill it; and (d) identify the resulting carcass and blood as inedible.

### Subdivision F — Slaughtering and Dressing

This Subdivision sets out the procedures that must be followed when slaughtering and dressing food animals, including requirements for rendering animals unconscious, ritual slaughter, and carcass dressing.

#### Requirement Before Bleeding (Section 141)

Before bleeding a food animal (excluding game animals), licence holders must render it unconscious in a manner preventing regained consciousness before death, by: (a) a mechanical head blow causing immediate unconsciousness; (b) an electrical current causing immediate unconsciousness; or (c) gas exposure causing rapid unconsciousness.

#### Requirement After Bleeding Starts (Section 142)

Licence holders must not cut a carcass after bleeding starts if the animal shows sensibility signs indicating possible return to consciousness.

#### Requirement Before Suspending (Section 143)

**General rule (subsection 143(1)):** Animals must not be suspended before being rendered unconscious/slaughtered under section 141, ritually slaughtered under section 144, or humanely killed.

**Exception for certain birds (subsection 143(2)):** Birds (other than ostrich, rhea, emu) may be suspended by the legs immediately before unconsciousness/slaughter or humane killing.

#### Ritual Slaughter Procedure (Section 144)

Despite section 141, ritual slaughter to comply with Judaic or Islamic law requires: (a) restraining the animal; (b) one continuous knife cut severing jugular veins and carotid arteries rapidly/simultaneously/completely, causing immediate bleeding; and (c) rapid, complete bleeding to render it unconscious without regaining consciousness before death.

#### Dressing the Carcass (Section 145)

**General procedure (subsection 145(1)):** After a food animal is bled, a licence holder must dress the carcass as follows:

- **(a) Pig carcasses:** (i) remove the hair, scurf, toenails and developed mammary glands, or remove the skin, head, developed mammary glands and feet at the carpal and tarsal joints, and (ii) eviscerate and split it.
- **(b) Bird carcasses:** (i) remove the feathers and hair, or remove the skin, (ii) remove the head, uropygial gland and feet at the tarsal joints, and (iii) eviscerate it.
- **(c) Goat carcasses:** (i) remove the hair, head, toenails and developed mammary glands, or remove the skin, head, developed mammary glands and feet at the carpal and tarsal joints, and (ii) eviscerate it.
- **(d) Carcasses of any other food animal:** (i) remove the skin, head, developed mammary glands and feet at the carpal and tarsal joints, (ii) eviscerate it, and (iii) split it, except in the case of a sheep, calf or domesticated rabbit.

**Partial dressing (subsection 145(2)):** Despite subsection (1), the Minister must authorize partial dressing at the licence holder's request if (a) the licence holder establishes a market exists for partially dressed carcasses, and (b) the partial dressing method leaves the carcass sufficiently dressed to allow post-mortem examination or inspection.

### 146 — Blood Clots, Bone Splinters and Extraneous Matter

A licence holder must remove any blood clot, bone splinter and extraneous matter from the carcass of a food animal and the parts of the carcass, and must identify what was removed as inedible.

### 147 — Processing of Blood

A licence holder must process a food animal's blood in the inedible products area described in subsection 58(2) unless the licence holder:

(a) collects it in a manner that prevents contamination;

(b) protects it against contamination after it is collected; and

(c) does not defibrinate it by hand.

### 148 — Identification and Correlation of Blood and Carcass Parts

A licence holder must identify the blood of a food animal that is collected to be processed as an edible meat product and the parts of the carcass of the food animal in a manner that enables the correlation of the blood and the parts with the carcass from which they were removed until the completion of the post-mortem inspection or examination.

### 149 — Post-mortem Inspection Requirements and Deviations

**Post-mortem inspection.** A licence holder must, during the course of dressing or partially dressing a carcass, present the carcass, its parts, and any blood of the food animal that is collected to be processed as an edible meat product to a veterinary inspector, or an inspector under the supervision of a veterinary inspector, for a post-mortem inspection.

**Deviations.** A licence holder, other than a licence holder who is authorized to conduct a post-mortem defect management program, must not, before the post-mortem inspection is completed, remove from the carcass any part that shows a deviation from normal appearance unless authorized to do so by a veterinary inspector.

**Deviations — post-mortem defect management program.** In the case of a licence holder who is authorized to conduct a post-mortem defect management program, the licence holder must not, before the post-mortem inspection begins, remove from the carcass any part that shows a deviation from normal appearance unless authorized to do so by a veterinary inspector.

**Exception — post-mortem examination program.** This section does not apply to a licence holder who is authorized to conduct a post-mortem examination program under subsection 160(3).

### 150 — Post-mortem Examination and Defect Management Program

**Post-mortem examination.** During the course of dressing or partially dressing a carcass, a licence holder who is authorized to conduct a post-mortem examination program under subsection 160(3) must, under the supervision of a veterinary inspector, conduct a post-mortem examination of the carcass, its parts and the blood of the food animal that has been collected to be processed as an edible meat product.

**Post-mortem defect management program.** During the course of dressing or partially dressing a carcass, a licence holder who is authorized to conduct a post-mortem defect management program under subsection 160(3) must, under the supervision of a veterinary inspector, do the following:

(a) before the post-mortem inspection begins, conduct a post-mortem screening of the carcass, its parts and the blood of the food animal that has been collected to be processed as an edible meat product; and

(b) before the post-mortem inspection of the carcass is completed, implement the necessary measures with respect to any defects of the carcass or its parts.

### 151 — Inspection Legend Applied Before Refrigeration

In the case of an edible dressed or partially dressed whole carcass or an edible dressed carcass side, other than a carcass or carcass side of a domesticated rabbit or a bird that is not an ostrich, rhea or emu, the inspection legend must be applied after the post-mortem inspection or examination and before refrigeration:

(a) by stamping it directly onto the carcass or carcass side; or

(b) by applying a label to the carcass or carcass side that is shown prominently and that bears the inspection legend, the date of slaughter of the food animal from which the carcass or carcass side is derived and a code that enables the carcass or carcass side to be correlated with the slaughter of the food animal.

### 152 — Condemnation of Meat Products

If a veterinary inspector, or an inspector under the supervision of a veterinary inspector, determines after a post-mortem inspection that any meat product that would be derived from a carcass, any of its parts or the blood of the food animal could not be identified as edible under section 125 and condemns the carcass, its parts or the blood of the food animal, a licence holder must identify any meat product that is derived from the condemned carcass, parts or blood as inedible.

### 153 — Identification of Inedible Carcasses and Parts

A licence holder must identify as inedible:

(a) the carcass, any of its parts or the blood of a food animal that is rejected by the licence holder who is authorized to conduct a post-mortem examination program or a post-mortem defect management program under subsection 160(3); and

(b) the carcass of a food animal that dies other than by slaughter in accordance with these Regulations.

### 154 — Meat Products Treated as Inedible

**Treatment as inedible.** A licence holder may treat as inedible any meat product that:

(a) does not have its movement restricted by an inspector; or

(b) has its movement restricted by an inspector if the licence holder obtains an inspector's authorization to move the meat product to the inedible products area described in subsection 58(2).

**Identification.** The licence holder must identify as inedible any meat product that the licence holder treats as inedible.

### 155 — Inedible Products Area, Labelling, Disposal and Specified Risk Material

**Inedible products area.** When a meat product is condemned or identified as inedible, a licence holder must move it directly to the inedible products area described in subsection 58(2).

**Labelling and disposal.** The licence holder must take one of the following measures in respect of a meat product that is moved to the inedible products area:

(a) apply or attach a label to it that indicates its intended use and bears the expression "Not for Use as Human Food" or "ne peut servir à l'alimentation humaine"; or

(b) dispose of it in accordance with section 66.

**Specified risk material.** Despite subsection (2), the licence holder must keep a meat product that is a specified risk material, contains a specified risk material or is derived from a specified risk material in a separate area of the inedible products area and must handle and destroy it in accordance with Part I.1 of the Health of Animals Regulations.

### 156 — Treatment of Contaminated Meat Product

A licence holder must take one of the following measures in respect of a contaminated meat product:

(a) subject it to a treatment or process that eliminates the contamination; or

(b) identify it as inedible.

### 157 — Trichinella spp. in Pork

A licence holder may identify as edible a meat product that is derived from a pig and that does not require further preparation before consumption, other than washing or thawing or exposing it to sufficient heat to warm it without cooking it, only if the conditions for identifying the meat product as edible under section 125 are met and:

(a) the pork is subjected to a treatment or process that inactivates Trichinella spp. viable larvae;

(b) the pork is derived from a carcass that tests negative for the detection of Trichinella spp. larvae using a method that is shown by evidence to be effective; or

(c) the pig originates from a farm that operates an on-farm food safety program under which the risk of Trichinella spp. infection is negligible.

### 158 — Trichinella spp. in Equine Meat

A licence holder may identify as edible a meat product that is derived from an equine only if the conditions for identifying the meat product as edible under section 125 are met and the equine's carcass tests negative for the detection of Trichinella spp. larvae using a method that is shown by evidence to be effective.

### 159 — Bovine Cysticercosis

A licence holder may identify as edible a meat product that is derived from a bovine whose carcass is affected by or shows evidence of bovine cysticercosis only if the conditions for identifying the meat product as edible under section 125 are met and the licence holder has:

(a) removed the parts of the carcass that are affected and identified them as inedible; and

(b) subjected the remaining parts to a treatment or process that inactivates bovine cysticercosis viable larvae.

### 160 — Application for Authorization to Conduct a Post-mortem Program

**Application for an authorization.** The holder of a licence to slaughter may apply in writing to the Minister for an authorization to conduct:

(a) a post-mortem examination program concerning a food animal referred to in the document entitled *Fundamentals of the Post-mortem Examination Program*, prepared by the Agency and published on its website, as amended from time to time; or

(b) a post-mortem defect management program concerning a food animal referred to in the document entitled *Fundamentals of the Post-mortem Defect Management Program* prepared by the Agency and published on its website, as amended from time to time.

**Contents of application.** The application must include:

(a) the licence holder's licence number and the address of the establishment where the program will be conducted; and

(b) a copy of the licence holder's preventive control plan that meets the requirements of section 89.

**Authorization.** The Minister must authorize the licence holder to conduct a post-mortem examination program or a post-mortem defect management program if:

(a) the preventive control plan that is included in the licence holder's application meets the requirements of section 89; and

(b) the Minister is of the opinion, based on the information that was made available to him or her, that the licence holder is able to conduct the program in compliance with these Regulations.

### 161 — Grounds for Suspension of Post-mortem Program Authorization

The Minister may suspend a licence holder's authorization to conduct a post-mortem examination program or a post-mortem defect management program if:

(a) the licence holder does not comply with their post-mortem examination program or post-mortem defect management program;

(b) the licence holder does not comply with any provision of the Act or of these Regulations; or

(c) the Minister is of the opinion that a risk of injury to human health may result if the licence holder continues to conduct the program.

### 162 — Suspension Procedure for Post-mortem Program Authorization

**Suspension.** The Minister must not suspend a licence holder's authorization to conduct a post-mortem examination program or a post-mortem defect management program unless the licence holder:

(a) was provided with an inspection report that sets out the grounds for the suspension and the period within which corrective action must be taken in order to avoid the suspension; and

(b) failed to take corrective action within that period.

**Written notice.** The Minister must notify the licence holder in writing of the suspension and the date on which it takes effect.

### 163 — Immediate Suspension Where Risk of Injury to Human Health

**Risk of injury to human health.** Despite section 162, if the Minister is of the opinion that a risk of injury to human health may result if the licence holder continues to conduct the post-mortem examination program or post-mortem defect management program, the Minister may suspend the authorization immediately after the licence holder is provided with an inspection report that sets out the grounds for the suspension.

**Written notice.** The Minister must notify the licence holder in writing that their authorization is suspended and that the suspension takes effect immediately.

### 164 — Lifting of Suspension for Post-mortem Program Authorization

The suspension of an authorization to conduct a post-mortem examination program or post-mortem defect management program must be lifted if the Minister determines that corrective action has been taken.

### 165 — Required Food Animal Information Documents for Equines and Birds

**Required documents.** Before slaughtering a food animal that is an equine or a bird, the holder of a licence to slaughter must obtain, from the person who owned or had the possession, care or control of the food animal before its arrival at the establishment where it is intended to be slaughtered, documents that include the following information:

(a) the name and contact information of the person who owned it and any person who had the possession, care or control of it immediately before its arrival at the establishment;

(b) the last location where it was raised or kept before its arrival at the establishment, including the address of the location, its code or the number that identifies it;

(c) the food animal's identification number or any other information that identifies it;

(d) the name of any on-farm food safety program under which the food animal was raised or kept;

(e) in the case of a bird:
- (i) the time at which the first bird of the lot of birds was placed into a crate,
- (ii) the time at which the bird was last provided with access to a source of hydration before being loaded, and
- (iii) the time at which the bird was last provided with access to feed before being loaded;

(f) a description of any physical or chemical hazard to which the food animal may have been exposed that could cause any meat product that is derived from the food animal to be contaminated;

(g) in respect of the last 120 days of the life of a bird that has been used for breeding or egg production or in respect of the entire life of any other bird:
- (i) the mortality rate for the flock from which the bird originates,
- (ii) the name of any disease or syndrome that was diagnosed in the flock from which the bird originates and the date on which the flock recovered from the disease or syndrome,
- (iii) the names of any drugs administered to treat a disease or syndrome or administered as an extra-label use and of any vaccines that were administered to the bird, including route of administration, first and last administration dates, dosage, and withdrawal period (or a prescription copy and attestation for extra-label use), and
- (iv) the name of any drug administered in the last 14 days that requires a withdrawal period; and

(h) in respect of the last 180 days of the life of an equine:
- (i) the name of any disease or syndrome diagnosed or a description of any deviation from normal behaviour, physiology or appearance,
- (ii) the names of any drugs and vaccines administered, including Drug Identification Numbers (if any), route of administration, last administration date, dosage, and withdrawal period (or prescription copy and attestation for extra-label use), and
- (iii) the use of the equine.

### 165(2)-(4) — Exceptions and Retention Period for Food Animal Information Documents

**Exception — equines and birds.** Despite subsection (1) and subsections 138(1) and 139(1), the licence holder may slaughter the equine or bird without having first obtained the documents referred to in subsection (1) if they notify an inspector before slaughtering the equine or bird and if, subsequent to the slaughter, the meat product that is derived from the equine or bird:

(a) is held by the licence holder until they have obtained those documents, examined them and presented them to a veterinary inspector or to an inspector under the supervision of a veterinary inspector; or

(b) is identified as inedible.

**Exception — game animal, ostrich, rhea and emu.** Subsection (1) does not apply in respect of a game animal or an ostrich, rhea or emu.

**Retention period of documents.** The documents referred to in subsection (1) must be kept for one year after the day on which the food animal arrives at the establishment.

### 166 — Document Keeping for Slaughtered and Deceased Food Animals

**Document keeping.** The holder of a licence to slaughter must prepare and keep documents that set out the following:

(a) in the case of a food animal that is slaughtered, the date and time that it was slaughtered and the findings of the ante-mortem examination and, if the licence holder is authorized to conduct a post-mortem examination program or a post-mortem defect management program, the findings of the post-mortem examination or the post-mortem screening and the reason for any condemnation or rejection;

(b) in the case of a food animal that is found dead at the time of its arrival at the establishment or that dies in the establishment other than by slaughter in accordance with these Regulations:
- (i) the date and time that the food animal was found dead or was humanely killed,
- (ii) its identification number or any other information that identifies it, and
- (iii) the details of its disposal; and

(c) with respect to the food animals referred to in paragraph (b), the total number per day.

**Retention period of documents.** The documents referred to in subsection (1) must be kept for one year after the day on which the food animal arrives at the establishment.

### 167 — Import of Edible Meat Products

The holder of a licence to import may import an edible meat product only if:

(a) the foreign state in which the meat product is manufactured, prepared, stored, packaged or labelled, as the case may be, has, at the time the activity is conducted, an inspection system for meat products that is recognized under Part 7;

(b) the foreign state from which the meat product is imported has, at the time of the import, an inspection system for meat products that is recognized under Part 7;

(c) the establishment where the food animal from which the meat product is derived was slaughtered, and any establishment where the meat product was manufactured, processed, treated, preserved, handled, tested, graded, coded, stored, packaged or labelled, have, at the time that the activity is conducted and at the time of the import, a system for those activities that is recognized under Part 7; and

(d) the licence holder provides an inspector with an official document issued by the foreign state, in a form approved by the President, that states that the meat product meets the requirements that are set out in the Act and these Regulations.

### 168 — Export of Edible Meat Products

**Export.** A licence holder may export an edible meat product only if:

(a) the licence holder provides an inspector with a document that substantiates that the requirements of the foreign state to which it is intended to be exported are met in respect of that meat product; and

(b) a certificate or other document is issued under section 48 of the Act in respect of that meat product.

**Exception — removal notice.** Subsection (1) does not apply in respect of a meat product that is subject to a notice ordering its removal from Canada under subsection 32(1) of the Act.

### 169 — Exception for Shellfish Under Part 7

This Part does not apply in respect of the adductor muscles of scallops and the meat of geoducks.

### 170 — Application for Recognition of a Foreign Inspection System

**Application for recognition of inspection system.** A foreign state may make an application to the Minister in writing for the recognition of its inspection system for meat products or live or raw shellfish.

**Contents of application.** The application must include the following information:

(a) in the case of an inspection system for meat products:
- (i) an indication of the species of birds or mammals and a description of the meat products to which the system applies, and
- (ii) the approximate number of establishments where the manufacturing, preparing, storing, packaging or labelling of meat products that are intended to be exported to Canada would be conducted and an indication of the activities that would be conducted in those establishments;

(b) in the case of an inspection system for live or raw shellfish, an indication of the species and the growing and harvesting areas to which the system applies;

(c) the volume of meat products or live or raw shellfish to which the system applies that is anticipated to be exported to Canada per year;

(d) the items set out in paragraph (3)(a) or (b), as the case may be; and

(e) the name, title and signature of the authorized representative of the foreign state who makes the application.

### 170(3) — Ministerial Recognition Criteria for Foreign Inspection Systems

The Minister must recognize the inspection system in respect of which the application is made if the system provides at least the same level of protection as that provided by the provisions of the Act and these Regulations, taking into account the following:

(a) in the case of an inspection system for meat products:
- (i) any applicable legislative framework, controls and procedures,
- (ii) the organizational structure of the authority responsible for the system,
- (iii) the implementation of the system,
- (iv) the resources that support the objectives of the system,
- (v) the humane treatment of the food animals intended to be slaughtered,
- (vi) the chemical residue monitoring and microbiological monitoring of the meat products,
- (vii) the certification process for the export of the meat products, and
- (viii) any other relevant information; and

(b) in the case of an inspection system for live or raw shellfish:
- (i) any applicable legislative framework, controls and procedures,
- (ii) the organizational structure of the authority responsible for the system,
- (iii) the implementation of the system,
- (iv) the resources that support the objectives of the system,
- (v) the chemical and microbiological monitoring of the shellfish, including monitoring for biotoxins,
- (vi) the monitoring of the waters in the growing and harvesting areas to assess their suitability for their intended purpose, and
- (vii) any other relevant information.

### 171 — Recognition of Establishment-Level Systems Subject to a Recognized Foreign Inspection System

**Application for recognition of system.** If a foreign state's inspection system for meat products is recognized, the foreign state may make an application to the Minister in writing for the recognition of the system of manufacturing, processing, treating, preserving, handling, testing, grading, coding, slaughtering, storing, packaging or labelling that is used in an establishment and that is subject to that inspection system.

**Contents of application.** The application must include the following information:

(a) the name of the person who conducts any relevant activities and the establishment's address;

(b) the establishment's registration number, or another identification number for the establishment, that is provided by the foreign state;

(c) a statement that identifies the system in respect of which the application is made;

(d) a declaration by the authorized representative of the foreign state who makes the application that states that the system in respect of which the application is made is subject to the foreign state's recognized inspection system and meets the requirements of that inspection system that apply to the conduct of those activities in respect of meat products that are intended to be exported to Canada; and

(e) the name, title and signature of the authorized representative of the foreign state who makes the application.

**Recognition by Minister.** The Minister must recognize a system referred to above if:

(a) the foreign state's inspection system for the relevant meat products is recognized under subsection 170(3); and

(b) the system is subject to the inspection system referred to in paragraph (a) and meets the requirements of that inspection system that apply to those activities in respect of meat products that are intended to be exported to Canada.

### 172 — Suspension of Recognition of Foreign Inspection Systems and Establishment Systems

**Suspension of recognition — inspection system.** The Minister must suspend the recognition of a foreign state's inspection system that is referred to in section 170 if:

(a) the foreign state fails to notify the Minister in writing, as soon as feasible, of any changes that it has made to the system or to the legislation governing the system; or

(b) the system no longer provides at least the same level of protection as that provided by the provisions of the Act and these Regulations.

**Suspension of recognition — system used in establishment.** The Minister must suspend the recognition of a system referred to in section 171 if:

(a) the system no longer meets the foreign state's requirements that apply to those activities;

(b) a shipment of meat products manufactured using the system is determined to contravene a provision of the Act, these Regulations, the Food and Drugs Act or the Food and Drug Regulations, and either two other shipments using the same system were found non-compliant in the prior six months, or two of the four most recent shipments using the system were found non-compliant;

(c) the system is no longer subject to the foreign state's inspection system; or

(d) the recognition of the foreign state's inspection system to which the system is subject has been suspended.

**Notice and effective date.** The Minister must notify the foreign state in writing of the suspension, the grounds for it, and the date on which it takes effect; the suspension takes effect on the day the notice is issued.

**Reinstatement of recognition.** The Minister must notify the foreign state in writing that the recognition is no longer suspended if the circumstances that gave rise to the suspension have been remedied, or, for a suspension based on a non-compliant shipment, once the establishment has taken corrective action.

### 173 — Cancellation of Recognition of Foreign Inspection Systems and Establishment Systems

**Cancellation of recognition — inspection system.** The Minister must cancel the recognition of a foreign state's inspection system referred to in section 170 if:

(a) no meat products or live or raw shellfish to which the system applies have been exported to Canada from the foreign state in the previous five years;

(b) the circumstances that gave rise to a suspension have not been remedied within 12 months after the day on which the recognition was suspended; or

(c) the foreign state requests the cancellation in writing.

**Cancellation of recognition — system used in establishment.** The Minister must cancel the recognition of a system referred to in section 171 if:

(a) the recognition of the inspection system to which it is subject has been cancelled;

(b) no meat products manufactured using the system have been exported to Canada from the foreign state in the previous five years;

(c) circumstances that gave rise to a suspension have not been remedied within 12 months after the day on which the recognition was suspended; or

(d) the foreign state in which the establishment is located requests the cancellation in writing.

**Notice and effective date.** The Minister must notify the foreign state in writing of the cancellation, the grounds for it, and the date on which it takes effect; the cancellation takes effect on the day the notice is issued.

### 173.1 — Definition of Test Market Food

For the purposes of section 174, *test market food* means a food that:

(a) prior to being subject to an exemption referred to in subsection 174(1), was not sold in Canada in its current form; and

(b) differs substantially from any other food sold in Canada with respect to its composition, function, condition or packaging form.

### 174 — Application for Ministerial Exemption: Test Market Food or Supply Shortage

**Application for exemption.** Any person may apply, in a form approved by the President, for an exemption from the application of a provision of the Act or these Regulations for the purpose of selling a test market food or for alleviating a shortage in Canada in the available supply of a food that is manufactured, processed or produced in Canada.

**Exemption.** The Minister may, in writing, grant the exemption if:

(a) the information submitted in the application is complete, truthful and not misleading;

(b) the food meets the requirements set out in paragraphs 8(1)(a) to (d);

(c) the Minister is of the opinion that no risk of injury to human health will result;

(d) in the case of a test market food exemption, the Minister is of the opinion that the exemption will not confuse or mislead the public, or disrupt normal trading or pricing patterns of the industry; and

(e) in the case of a supply-shortage exemption, the exemption is necessary to alleviate that shortage.

### 175 — Application for Ministerial Exemption: Inspection Legend

**Application for exemption.** The holder of a licence to slaughter food animals and to process meat products may apply, in a form approved by the President, for an exemption from the application of section 151 in respect of a carcass or carcass side.

**Exemption.** The Minister may, in writing, grant the exemption if:

(a) the information submitted is complete, truthful and not misleading;

(b) the licence holder has established procedures for processing the carcass or carcass side within the establishment where the food animal was slaughtered, and for identifying it to allow linkage with derived edible meat products, with documents kept for this purpose;

(c) the Minister is of the opinion that no risk of injury to human health will result; and

(d) the Minister is of the opinion that no risk of harm to interprovincial trade or export will result.

**Conditions.** The exemption is subject to the conditions that the carcass or carcass side must be processed within the establishment where the food animal was slaughtered, and must be identified and documented to allow linkage with derived edible meat products.

### 176-178 — Additional Conditions, Validity Period and Cancellation of Ministerial Exemptions

**Additional conditions.** The Minister may, at any time, make any exemption referred to in subsection 174(2) or 175(2) subject to conditions.

**Period of validity.** An exemption referred to in subsection 174(2) or 175(2) is valid until the expiry date specified in the exemption or, if no date is specified, until the end of the period that is two years after the day on which the exemption is granted.

**Cancellation.** The Minister may cancel an exemption if:

(a) the Minister is of the opinion that not cancelling it may result in a risk of injury to human health;

(b) in the case of an exemption referred to in subsection 175(2), the Minister is of the opinion that not cancelling it may result in a risk of harm to interprovincial trade or export; or

(c) the person who has been granted the exemption does not comply with any condition to which it is subject, or with any other provision of the Act or these Regulations.

### 179 — Definition of Inspection Mark for Inspection Legends

The inspection legends that are set out in Figures 1 and 2 of Schedule 2 are prescribed for the purposes of the definition *inspection mark* in section 2 of the Act.

### 180 — Authorized Use of the Inspection Legend for Edible Meat Products

**Edible meat products — Figure 1 of Schedule 2.** A licence holder or an inspector is authorized to apply the inspection legend set out in Figure 1 of Schedule 2 to, and use it in connection with, an edible meat product, whether prepackaged or not, if the following conditions are met:

(a) the meat product was manufactured, processed, treated, preserved, packaged or labelled by the licence holder in accordance with the Act and these Regulations;

(b) any meat product it contains that was manufactured, processed, treated, preserved, packaged or labelled in Canada was done so by a licence holder in compliance with the Act and these Regulations;

(c) if it is a graded livestock or poultry carcass (or derived from one), grading was conducted by a grader in accordance with these Regulations;

(d) any imported meat product it contains was imported by a licence holder in compliance with the Act and these Regulations;

(e) if derived from food animals slaughtered in Canada, the slaughter was conducted by a licence holder in compliance with the Act and these Regulations;

(f) the meat product complies with Volume 7 of the Standards of Identity Document and the requirements in paragraphs 8(1)(a) to (d) and Division 7 of Part 6; and

(g) the inspection legend is applied or used in the establishment identified in the licence holder's licence, unless the product is intended for export and the legend is applied to or used in connection with the exporting conveyance.

### 180(2)-(4) — Figure 2 Inspection Legend, Export Exception and Beef Carcass Exception

**Edible meat products — Figure 2 of Schedule 2.** A licence holder or an inspector is authorized to apply the inspection legend set out in Figure 2 of Schedule 2 to a prepackaged edible meat product if the conditions in subsection (1) are met and the container:

(a) is a hermetically sealed package labelled legibly and permanently to identify the establishment in the licence holder's licence;

(b) is a casing or bag closed by a clip bearing a legibly engraved establishment number visible when closed; or

(c) bears the establishment number on the label, except on any part applied to the bottom of the container.

**Exception — export under section 16.** For meat products exported under subsection 16(1), the requirements of subsections (1) and (2) must be met except for paragraph (1)(f) where that is the unmet requirement referred to in subsection 16(1).

**Exception — dressed beef carcass side.** Despite paragraph (1)(f), a licence holder may apply the Figure 1 inspection legend, after post-mortem inspection and before refrigeration, to a dressed beef carcass side containing dorsal root ganglia if the carcass side is clearly marked as containing them. The dorsal root ganglia must be removed before any derived meat product is identified as edible.

### 181 — Inspection Legend for Processed Egg Products

A licence holder or an inspector is authorized to apply the inspection legend set out in Figure 1 of Schedule 2 to a prepackaged processed egg product if:

(a) it was manufactured, processed, treated, preserved, packaged or labelled by the licence holder in compliance with the Act and these Regulations;

(b) any such product made in Canada that it contains was processed by a licence holder in compliance with the Act and these Regulations;

(c) any eggs from which it is derived that were processed, treated, preserved, graded, packaged or labelled in Canada were done so by a licence holder in compliance with the Act and these Regulations;

(d) it complies with Volume 2 of the Standards of Identity Document and the requirements in paragraphs 8(1)(a) to (d) and Division 4 of Part 6; and

(e) the inspection legend is applied or used in an establishment identified in the licence holder's licence.

### 182 — Inspection Legend for Fish

A licence holder or an inspector is authorized to apply the inspection legend set out in Figure 1 or 2 of Schedule 2 to prepackaged fish if:

(a) it was manufactured, processed, treated or preserved by the licence holder in compliance with the Act and these Regulations;

(b) any fish it contains that was manufactured, processed, treated, preserved, graded, packaged or labelled in Canada was done so by a licence holder in compliance with the Act and these Regulations; and

(c) it complies with Volume 3 of the Standards of Identity Document and the requirements in paragraphs 8(1)(a) to (d) and Division 5 of Part 6.

### 183 — Establishment Number Replacing "00" on the Inspection Legend

A licence holder or an inspector who applies or uses the inspection legend set out in Figure 1 of Schedule 2 must replace the numbers "00" with the number identifying the licence holder's establishment.

### 184 — Authorized Use of Inspection Legends by Printers, Publishers and Manufacturers

**Authorized use.** The following persons are authorized to use the inspection legends set out in Figures 1 and 2 of Schedule 2:

(a) printers of labels (other than official export label printers) and manufacturers of packages, if provided to a person authorized under sections 180 to 182;

(b) printers of official export labels, if the labels are provided to an inspector;

(c) publishers of documents on the inspection of meat products, processed egg products or fish;

(d) publishers of documents that advertise meat products, processed egg products or fish; and

(e) manufacturers of stamps, if provided to a person authorized under sections 180 to 182.

**Advertising and sale.** Any person authorized under subsection (1) to use an inspection legend is also authorized to advertise and sell the labels, packages, documents and stamps bearing it.

### 185 — Advertising and Sale of Foods Bearing an Inspection Legend

Any person is authorized to advertise and sell a meat product, prepackaged processed egg product or prepackaged fish to which an inspection legend has been applied or used, if it was applied or used in accordance with these Regulations. This includes using an inspection legend set out in Figures 1 and 2 of Schedule 2 for advertising those products.

### 185.1 — Minimum Character Height for Inspection Legend Used as a Label

In the case of the inspection legend set out in Figure 1 of Schedule 2, if it is used or is to be used as a label, the number identifying the licence holder's establishment that replaces the numbers "00" must be in characters of at least 1.6 mm in height.

### 186 — General Packaging Requirements for Prepackaged Food

A prepackaged food that is sent or conveyed from one province to another or that is imported or exported must meet the following requirements:

(a) its package must:
- (i) be suitable for its intended use and appropriate for the food,
- (ii) protect the food against moisture, loss, damage, contamination and deterioration during normal handling, storing and conveying,
- (iii) be clean and in sanitary condition,
- (iv) be of sound construction,
- (v) be free from odours that might affect the food,
- (vi) not impart any undesirable substance to the food,
- (vii) not have a design, mark or colour that enhances the food's apparent quality or composition, and
- (viii) be new, in the case of certain egg-related liners, packages, cartons and trays;

(b) processed egg product packages that have previously been used and are not corrosion-resistant must be lined with a sanitary plastic or equivalent liner;

(c) reused plastic trays for graded eggs must be sanitized and dry before reuse; and

(d) packages for Canada A or Canada B graded eggs must not have previously held ungraded or Canada Nest Run eggs.

### 187 — Application of Standard Container Size Requirements

The requirements of this Division apply in respect of any food that is sent or conveyed from one province to another or imported.

### 188 — Standard Container Sizes and Exceptions

**Size corresponding to net quantity.** The container of a prepackaged food, including a hermetically sealed food container listed in the Standard Container Sizes Document, must be of a size that corresponds to a net quantity by weight or volume, or maximum capacity by net weight, as set out in that document; metal hermetically sealed containers must match the dimensions corresponding to the net volume in that document.

**Exception — catch-weight foods.** Subsection (1) does not apply to a consumer prepackaged catch-weight food if a label bearing the net weight for retail sale is applied or attached.

**Exception — consumer prepackaged foods.** Subsection (1) does not apply to a consumer prepackaged food that is:

(a) manufactured, prepared, produced, packaged or labelled for commercial, industrial or institutional use without being sold by them as a consumer prepackaged food;

(b) manufactured, prepared, produced, packaged or labelled only for sale to or by a duty-free shop; or

(c) distributed to one or more persons for no consideration.

### 194-195 — Fill Standards for Frozen Processed Fruit or Vegetable Products

**Application.** The requirements of this Division apply in respect of any foods that are sent or conveyed from one province to another, imported or exported.

**Frozen processed fruit or vegetable products.** At least 90% of the volume capacity of the package of a frozen processed fruit or vegetable product must be filled with the product.

### 196-197 — Fill Standards for Non-frozen and Hermetically Sealed Processed Fruit or Vegetable Products

**Non-frozen processed fruit or vegetable products.** The package of a processed fruit or vegetable product that is not frozen:

(a) must be filled with as much of the product as its preparation allows; and

(b) must not contain more syrup, brine, water or other liquid packaging medium than is necessary for processing.

**Hermetically sealed packages.** Despite section 196, a processed fruit or vegetable product in a hermetically sealed package must meet the minimum and average drained weight requirements set out in the document entitled *Minimum Drained Weights and Average Drained Weights for Processed Fruit or Vegetable Products in a Hermetically Sealed Package*, prepared by the Agency and published on its website, as amended from time to time.

### 198 — Definitions for Part 11 (Labelling)

The following definitions apply in this Part.

*Canadian unit* means a unit of measurement that is set out in Schedule II to the Weights and Measures Act.

*metric unit* means a unit of measurement that is set out in Schedule I to the Weights and Measures Act.

### 199 — False, Misleading or Deceptive Labelling, Selling, Importing and Advertising

**Labelling.** For the purposes of subsection 6(1) of the Act, labelling a food in a manner that is false, misleading or deceptive or likely to create an erroneous impression includes labelling a food with:

(a) any representation that may reasonably be considered to qualify the declared net quantity of a consumer prepackaged food, or that is likely to deceive with respect to net quantity; or

(b) any expression that may reasonably be considered to imply that a prepackaged food contains matter it does not contain, or does not contain matter it does contain.

**Selling, importing and advertising.** For the purposes of subsection 6(1) of the Act, selling, importing or advertising a food in a manner that is false, misleading or deceptive includes selling, importing or advertising a food labelled in the manner set out in paragraph (1)(a) or (1)(b).

**Declaration of net quantity.** Labelling, selling, importing or advertising a consumer prepackaged food with a declaration of net quantity does not constitute false, misleading or deceptive labelling if the declaration meets any requirements under the Act and the actual net quantity is not less than the declared net quantity, subject to the tolerance under subsection (5).

**Tolerance.** The tolerance for a declared net quantity set out in column 1 of the applicable table in Schedule 4 is the tolerance set out in column 2 or 3 for that net quantity.

### 200 — Inspection of Net Quantity by Sampling

**Inspection — net quantity.** An inspection of a lot of food, each unit of which purports to contain the same net quantity, to determine compliance with paragraphs 199(3)(a) and (b) must be conducted by collecting and examining a sample from the lot.

**Number of units collected.** Subject to subsection (3), the inspector must collect at least the number of units set out in column 2 of Part 1 of Schedule 5 for the lot size set out in column 1; the units collected constitute the sample.

**Destruction of units.** If destruction of units is necessary to determine net quantity (other than to establish container weight), the inspector must not collect more than 10% of the total units in the lot, nor fewer than one unit, for destruction.

**Determination of inspector.** The lot does not meet the conditions of paragraphs 199(3)(a) and (b) if the inspector determines that the weighted average quantity is less than declared, that too many units fall short of the declared quantity by more than the prescribed tolerance, or that two or more units fall short by more than twice the prescribed tolerance.

**Liquid and frozen liquid foods.** For liquid consumer prepackaged foods, net quantity is determined assuming the liquid is at 20°C. For frozen liquid foods normally sold and consumed frozen, net quantity must be determined while frozen.

### 201-202 — Common Names and Standards; Icewine

**Common names.** A food, whether prepackaged or not, that is sent or conveyed interprovincially or imported or exported, and whose label bears a common name printed in boldface (not italic) type in the Standards of Identity Document, must meet any standard that applies to that common name.

**Icewine.** It is prohibited to sell a consumer prepackaged food labelled "icewine," "ice wine," "ice-wine," "vin de glace," or any similar word, abbreviation, symbol or phonetic rendering, unless the food meets the standard set out in Volume 8 of the Standards of Identity Document.

### 203 — Compliance with Labelling Requirements for All Shown Information

**Compliance with requirements of this Part.** An item of information shown on the label of a prepackaged food must meet any requirement of this Part that applies to it, even if these Regulations do not require that item of information to be shown.

**Application.** This applies to prepackaged foods sold in Canada or sent, conveyed or imported interprovincially (for Division 2 items), and to foods sent, conveyed, imported or exported interprovincially (for other items in these Regulations).

**Application — paragraphs 218(1)(a) and (b).** This also applies to dairy products, eggs, processed egg products, fish, fresh fruits or vegetables, processed fruit or vegetable products, honey, maple products or meat products that are prepackaged and exported.

### 204 — Use of the Word "Classifié"

If a provision of these Regulations requires the word "classifié" to be shown on a label, the word "classé" may be used in its place.

### 205 — Official Languages for Non-Consumer Prepackaged Food Labels

**Prepackaged food.** Any information required by these Regulations to be shown on the label of a prepackaged food, other than a consumer prepackaged food, and not referred to in subsection (3), must be shown in at least one official language.

**Exception — words or expressions in quotation marks.** For the purposes of subsection 48(2), section 254 and subsections 256(1) and 333(2), both an English and a French word or expression appearing in quotation marks must be shown, except where the label is authorized to show information in only one official language under subsections B.01.012(2) to (10) of the Food and Drug Regulations.

**Exception — both official languages required.** The label must bear, in both official languages, the size designation of Canada A graded eggs (section 316), and the colour class of Canada Grade A maple syrup (section 325).

### 206-207 — Official Languages for Consumer Prepackaged Food Labels

**Consumer prepackaged food.** Information required by these Regulations on the label of a consumer prepackaged food must be shown in both official languages, except where authorized otherwise under subsections B.01.012(2) to (10) of the Food and Drug Regulations.

**Words or expressions in quotation marks.** A provision requiring quoted words or expressions on a consumer prepackaged food label (other than paragraphs 110(a) and 111(a)) requires both English and French versions, subject to the same exception.

**Exception — fish grade name.** Despite subsection (1), the grade name may be shown on consumer prepackaged fish in only one official language.

**Modifications.** For the purposes of subsections 205(2) and 206(1) and (2), specific cross-references to the Food and Drug Regulations are read as references to Part 11 of these Regulations, with corresponding adaptations to terminology.

### 208-209 — Legibility, Type Size and Case Requirements

**Legibility.** Any information that a label is required to bear must be clearly and prominently shown and readily discernible to the purchaser under customary conditions of purchase and use.

**Upper or lower case.** A quoted word or expression required on a label may, unless otherwise provided, be shown in upper case, lower case, or both, provided it meets legibility and character height requirements.

### 210-211 — Character Height and Measurement Rules

**General rule.** Unless another provision specifies a character height, the information required on a consumer prepackaged food label must be shown in characters at least 1.6 mm in height.

**Exception for small packages.** That information (other than net quantity) may be shown in characters at least 0.8 mm in height if all Division 2 information appears on the principal display panel and the principal display surface area is 10 cm² or less.

**Measurement of type size.** Character height is measured using the height of an upper case letter (if shown in upper case only) or the height of the lower case letter "o" (if shown in lower case or mixed case).

### 212-213 — Application and Exceptions for Prepackaged Food Labelling Requirements

**Sale, interprovincial trade or import.** The requirements of this Division apply to any prepackaged food sold in Canada, sent or conveyed interprovincially, or imported.

**Export.** Section 217, paragraphs 218(1)(a) and (b), and section 225 also apply to dairy products, eggs, processed egg products, fish, fresh fruits or vegetables, processed fruit or vegetable products, honey, maple products or meat products that are prepackaged and exported.

**Exception — sections 214 and 217.** Sections 214 and 217 do not apply to one-bite confections sold individually, or to fresh fruits or vegetables packaged in a narrow wrapper/band (under 13 mm) or in a transparent protective wrapper or bag.

### 214-216.1 — Sale, Advertising and Expiration Date Prohibitions

**Sale — prepackaged food.** It is prohibited to sell a prepackaged food unless a compliant label is applied or attached.

**Advertising — consumer prepackaged food.** It is prohibited to advertise a consumer prepackaged food unless a compliant label is applied or attached.

**Representations relating to net quantity.** It is prohibited, in advertising a consumer prepackaged food, to represent net quantity except as set out in Divisions 1 and 2.

**Sale after expiration date.** It is prohibited to sell a prepackaged food after the expiration date required on its label under Divisions 24 and 25 of Part B of the Food and Drug Regulations, or to sell it unpackaged, or use it as an ingredient in another food.

### 217-218 — Label Required and Core Information for Prepackaged Foods

**Label required.** A prepackaged food must have a label meeting the requirements of these Regulations applied or attached in the manner set out in these Regulations.

**Prepackaged foods — label.** Unless otherwise provided, a label applied or attached to a prepackaged food must bear:

(a) the common name of the food, shown on the principal display panel;

(b) the name and principal place of business of the person by or for whom the food was manufactured, prepared, produced, stored, packaged or labelled, on any part of the label other than the bottom; and

(c) any other information required under the Food and Drug Regulations for a prepackaged product as defined in subsection B.01.001(1) of those Regulations.

**Exception — name and principal place of business.** The information in paragraph (1)(b) may be shown on the bottom-applied part of the label if it is also shown elsewhere on the label.

### 219-220 — Common Name Exceptions for Fresh Fruits, Vegetables and Apples

**Exception — common name.** The common name is not required on:

(a) prepackaged fresh fruits or vegetables packaged so they are visible and identifiable in the container; and

(b) consumer prepackaged fresh apples packaged so the variety name shows on any part of the label except the bottom.

**Definition of apple.** For paragraph (1)(b), *apple* means a fresh apple for which a grade is prescribed by these Regulations.

**Exception — name and principal place of business.** Consumer prepackaged fresh fruits or vegetables packaged at retail so they are visible and identifiable in the container are not required to bear the name and principal place of business referred to in paragraph 218(1)(b).

### 221-222 — Net Quantity Declaration and Place of Manufacture References

**Consumer prepackaged food — declaration of net quantity.** A label applied or attached to a consumer prepackaged food must bear, on the principal display panel, a declaration of net quantity.

**Place of manufacture — label or container.** If the label references, directly or indirectly, the place of manufacture of the label or container itself, that reference must be accompanied by a statement clarifying that it relates only to the label or container, not the food.

### 223 — Name of Importer and Geographic Origin Requirements

**Name of importer.** If a consumer prepackaged food was wholly manufactured, processed or produced in a foreign state, and the label shows the name and place of business of a Canadian person for whom or by whom it was stored, packaged or labelled, that information must be preceded by "Imported by"/"importé par" or "Imported for"/"importé pour," unless geographic origin is shown per subsection (3).

**Food packaged in Canada.** A similar requirement applies where wholly foreign-made food is packaged in Canada (other than at retail) and the Canadian packager's information is shown.

**Geographic origin.** Subject to other federal or provincial law, the geographic origin of a food must be shown in close proximity to, and in characters at least as tall as, the name and principal place of business of the manufacturer, processor or producer.

### 224 — Flavouring Ingredient Disclosure Requirements

**Statement required.** If a flavouring ingredient is added to a consumer prepackaged food, the label must indicate that it is imitation, artificial or simulated if the ingredient is not derived from a natural substance (meat, fish, poultry, fruits, vegetables, edible yeast, herbs, spices, etc.) and the label bears a pictorial representation suggesting the corresponding natural flavour.

**Placement of statement.** The statement must be shown on or near the pictorial representation if it's on the principal display panel; on the principal display panel near the common name if the representation is elsewhere; or on the principal-display-panel portion of the representation if it appears in both places.

### 225-226 — Requirement to Apply or Attach Labels

**Prepackaged food.** The label of a prepackaged food must be applied or attached so that it remains so at the time of sale.

**Consumer prepackaged food — container.** Subject to section 228, the label of a consumer prepackaged food for sale must be applied or attached to the container in accordance with section 227.

### 227-228 — Principal Display Surface, Ornamental Containers and Display Cards

**Principal display surface.** All or part of the label of a consumer prepackaged food must be applied to the principal display surface.

**Ornamental container.** Despite subsection (1), if the container is ornamental, the label may be applied to the bottom or attached to the container.

**Display card.** If the container is mounted on a display card, the label may be applied to the surface of the card that is displayed or visible under customary conditions of sale or use.

### 229 — Type Size for Net Quantity and Flavouring Statements on Consumer Prepackaged Food

**General rule.** The numerical quantity in the net quantity declaration, and the flavouring-ingredient statement under section 224, must be shown in characters at least the minimum height set out in column 2 of Schedule 6 for the principal display surface area in column 1.

**Display card — specific case.** For containers mounted on display cards, "Area of Principal Display Surface" is read as the total area of the display card surface that is displayed or visible under customary conditions of sale or use.

**Consumer prepackaged wine — specific case.** For 750 mL wine in a container no taller than 360 mm with a principal display surface greater than 258 cm², the numerical quantity may be shown smaller than the Schedule 6 minimum, but not less than 3.3 mm.

**Consumer prepackaged fresh fruits or vegetables — specific case.** Despite paragraph (1)(a), the numerical net quantity for these products must be shown in characters at least 1.6 mm in height.

### 230-231 — Legibility of Net Quantity Declaration and Units of Measurement

**Legibility.** The declaration of net quantity on a consumer prepackaged food label must be in distinct contrast to other label information and show the numerical quantity in boldface type.

**General requirements for declaration.** The declaration must be shown by volume, weight or numerical count in accordance with the document entitled *Units of Measurement for the Net Quantity Declaration of Certain Foods* (for listed foods), or, for unlisted foods, by volume (liquid/gas/viscous) or weight (solid), or per established trade practice if that practice differs.

### 232-235 — Metric Unit Rules for Net Quantity Declarations

**Permitted units.** The declaration of net quantity must be shown in metric units, unless otherwise provided.

**Millilitres, litres, grams and kilograms.** Units must be millilitres (under 1,000 mL), litres (1,000 mL or more), grams (under 1,000 g), or kilograms (1,000 g or more); 500 mL/g may be shown as 0.5 L/kg.

**Decimal fraction.** Net weight may be shown as a decimal fraction of a kilogram for foods packaged from bulk at retail or sold as catch-weight foods by a retailer.

**Number of digits.** Metric declarations must use three significant figures in decimal form, except quantities below 100 g/mL may use two figures; trailing zeros after the decimal point need not be shown.

**Quantity less than one.** If the quantity is less than one metric unit, it must be shown in words or in decimal form with a leading zero before the decimal point.

### 236-238 — Grouping of Metric and Canadian Units; Oysters

**Grouping.** If net quantity is shown in both metric and Canadian units, the units must be grouped together, except that safety symbols or pictograms under the Canada Consumer Product Safety Act may appear between them.

**Canadian units of volume.** For consumer prepackaged food under one gallon, Canadian units must be in fluid ounces, with specific equivalences permitted for pints, quarts, and half-gallons.

**Oysters.** Despite subsection (1), oysters sold in the shell (other than in hermetically sealed packages), if declared by volume, must use bushels or pecks.

### 239 — Net Quantity Declarations for Individually Packaged Foods Sold as One Unit

**General rule.** If a consumer prepackaged food is sold as one unit but consists of two or more individually packaged foods bearing their own required labelling, the overall declaration of net quantity must show the number of individually packaged foods in each class along with the common name, and either the total net quantity per class or the net quantity of each identical item in the class.

**Dairy products sold as one unit.** This does not apply to dairy products sold as one unit consisting of multiple individual packages (e.g., butter patties) with a total net quantity of 20 g or less.

**Prepackaged honey.** For graded prepackaged honey sold as one unit consisting of multiple individual packages, the label must bear the number of packages and net quantity of each, in metric units.

### 240-241 — Declarations of Servings on Consumer Prepackaged Food

**Prohibition — representation respecting number of servings.** It is prohibited to apply or attach a label representing the number of servings unless the label also bears a declaration of net quantity of each serving in accordance with section 241.

**Servings — placement and units.** The net quantity declaration of a serving must be shown in close proximity to, and in the same character height as, the servings representation, and must follow the same units rules as the main declaration (sections 231, 233-237), in metric units unless otherwise provided.

**Representation — cups or tablespoons.** If servings are expressed in cups or tablespoons, one cup equals 250 mL and one tablespoon equals 15 mL, and the metric-unit requirement does not apply.

**Exception — nutrition facts table.** Subsection (1) does not apply if the food bears a nutrition facts table containing the serving size in accordance with the Food and Drug Regulations.

### 241.1-241.5 — Additional Exceptions to Net Quantity Declaration Requirements

**Net quantity in advertisement.** If net quantity (or per-serving quantity) is shown in both metric and Canadian units on the label, an advertisement may show it in either unit.

**Exceptions to declaration of net quantity.** In addition to section 299 exceptions, no net quantity declaration is required for: individual portions sold via vending machine or mobile canteen; catch-weight foods sold to a retailer; individual restaurant portions served with meals/snacks; consumer prepackaged fresh fruits or vegetables sold by weight in an unsealed transparent bag at retail; and fresh fruits or vegetables in a confining band or transparent container of six or fewer, clearly visible at sale.

**Consumer prepackaged raspberries or strawberries.** Sections 216, 221 and 240 do not apply to raspberries or strawberries field-packaged in containers of 1.14 L capacity or less.

**Individually measured food.** Individually measured foods (measured/packaged in varying, non-fixed quantities) are exempt from certain legibility and character-height requirements. Foods packaged from bulk at retail (other than individually measured food) are also exempt from those requirements and from the metric-units requirement if clearly shown in Canadian units on the principal display panel.

**Individually packaged food sold as one unit — six or fewer.** Sections 221, 239 and 240 do not apply to a consumer prepackaged food consisting of six or fewer identical individually packaged foods sold as one unit, if each individual package is properly labelled and clearly visible at sale.

### 242-244 — Application and General Declaration Rules for Specific Food Requirements

**Application.** The requirements of this Division apply to any food sent, conveyed, imported or exported interprovincially.

**Exception — consumer prepackaged food.** The net quantity declaration requirements in this Division do not apply to consumer prepackaged food.

**Declaration of net quantity.** Any required declaration under this Division must be shown by volume, weight or numerical count in accordance with the *Units of Measurement for the Net Quantity Declaration of Certain Foods* document.

### 244.1-244.5 — Net Quantity Declaration Requirements for Specific Commodity Categories

**Foods requiring declaration.** The label of the following prepackaged foods must bear a declaration of net quantity: dairy products; graded eggs; fish; fresh fruits or vegetables; processed fruit or vegetable products; graded honey; maple products (except ungraded maple syrup); and edible meat products.

**Metric units and exceptions.** The declaration must generally be in metric units, except dairy products with a Volume 1 standard may use metric, Canadian, or both (grouped); fresh fruits or vegetables may use metric, Canadian, or both (grouped) unless declared by numerical count.

**Principal display panel requirement.** The net quantity declaration must appear on the principal display panel for prepackaged dairy products, hermetically sealed/commercially sterile fish, prepackaged processed fruit or vegetable products, and prepackaged edible meat products.

**Prepackaged edible meat products.** For these products, the declaration must follow the manner required by sections 233 to 236 and 239, as if the meat product were a consumer prepackaged food.

### 245 — Location of Required Information on Labels Under Division 3

**Food or container.** A label bearing information required by this Division must be applied or attached to the container (for prepackaged food) or to the food itself (for non-prepackaged food).

**Any part of label.** The information may appear on any part of the label, unless otherwise specified.

**Bottom of food or container.** If shown on a part of the label applied to the bottom, the information must also be duplicated elsewhere on the label where required by another provision, or otherwise on a non-bottom part.

### 246 — Required Information on Prepackaged Dairy Product Labels

The principal display panel of a prepackaged dairy product must bear:

(a) for butter, calorie-reduced butter, light/lite butter, dairy spread and whey butter: "Cultured"/"de culture" if prepared from cultured cream; "Whipped"/"fouetté" if air or inert gas was whipped in; "Unsalted"/"non salé" if unsalted and not cultured; and "Salted"/"salé" if both salted and cultured;

(b) for a combination of skim milk powder and whey powder, the percentage of each; and

(c) for partly skimmed milk powder, dairy spread and calorie-reduced butter, the percentage of milk fat.

### 247 — Additional Labelling for Non-Consumer Prepackaged Dairy Products

The principal display panel of a prepackaged dairy product, other than a consumer prepackaged one, must bear:

(a) for cheese in its original shape made from pasteurized milk, the word "Pasteurized"/"pasteurisé," unless the ingredient list already indicates this;

(b) for buttermilk powder, the percentage of milk fat;

(c) for skim milk powder with whey protein nitrogen content of at least 6.0 mg/g, "Low Heat"/"Low Temperature"/"basse température" (or abbreviated); and

(d) for skim milk powder with whey protein nitrogen content of at most 1.5 mg/g, "High Heat"/"High Temperature"/"haute température" (or abbreviated).

### 248-249 — Required Information on Consumer Prepackaged Dairy Products and Cheese

**Consumer prepackaged dairy products.** The principal display panel must bear: for cheese (except cottage/creamed cottage cheese) and cheese curd, the percentage of moisture; for cheese, cheese curd and evaporated/concentrated partly skimmed milk, the percentage of milk fat; and, for dairy products made wholly or partly from non-cow milk, the source of that milk (unless indicated in the common name).

**Consumer prepackaged cheese.** The principal display panel must also bear the relative firmness, the principal ripening characteristic (except for soft white cheese), and, for grated/shredded cheese mixtures, the cheese varieties in descending proportion order — subject to exceptions for cheddar, cream cheese, whey cheese, processed cheese (and its food/spread variants), cold-pack cheese (and its food variant), cottage cheese, creamed cottage cheese, and certain cheeses listed in the Food Compositional Standards Document.

**Relative firmness and ripening characteristic.** These must be identified using the expressions corresponding to the applicable moisture-on-fat-free-basis content or ripening process set out in the Descriptive Words, Expressions and Identification Names Document.

### 250-251 — Imported Dairy Products and Exceptions for Individual Portions

**Imported dairy products.** The label of an imported prepackaged dairy product, or of consumer prepackaged cheese packaged in Canada from imported bulk cheese with a Volume 1 standard, must bear "Product of"/"produit de" followed by the foreign state of origin; for the cheese case, this must appear on the principal display panel.

**Exception.** Subsection 239(2) and sections 244.3, 246, 248 and 250 do not apply to individual portions of consumer prepackaged dairy products sold via vending machine/mobile canteen or by a restaurant served with meals or snacks.

### 253-254 — Type Size for Imported Dairy Origin Statement; Pasteurized Graded Eggs

**Type size.** The "Product of" information required by section 250 must be shown in boldface characters at least 16 mm in height, for non-consumer prepackaged dairy products.

**Graded eggs — pasteurized.** The label of prepackaged eggs graded under these Regulations and pasteurized in the shell must bear "Pasteurized"/"pasteurisé" along with "Graded Canada A Before Pasteurization"/"classifié Canada A avant pasteurisation" or the Grade A equivalent, as applicable.

### 256 — Imported Egg Labelling and Character Height

**Imported eggs.** The label of imported prepackaged eggs must bear "Product of"/"produit de" followed by the name of the foreign state of origin.

**Location and type size.** This information must appear on the top or side of a tray/overwrap or egg carton in characters at least 1.5 mm in height, or in characters at least 6 mm in height on any other container.

### 258-260 — Inspection Legends and Origin Statements for Processed Egg Products

**Prepackaged processed egg products.** The label must bear the Figure 1 Schedule 2 inspection legend (if sent interprovincially or exported) or the foreign state's official inspection mark (if imported).

**Imported prepackaged processed egg products.** The label must also bear "Product of"/"produit de" followed by the name of the foreign state of origin.

**Prepackaged dried egg blends.** Dried whole egg, dried yolk, or dried egg white/albumen that is a blend of imported and Canadian product must bear "Product of Canada and"/"produit du Canada et" followed by the foreign state of origin.

### 261-263 — Salted Fish Definition and Labelling; Tuna Colour; Second Containers

**Definition of salted fish.** *Salted fish* means fish of the Gadidae family preserved by salt, with a salt content of 12% or more by wet weight and moisture content of not more than 65%.

**Prepackaged fish requirements.** The label must bear, for bivalve molluscs in the shell not hermetically sealed, the processing date and an expression/code/identifier of the harvest area; for hermetically sealed tuna, an expression describing the flesh colour per the Descriptive Words, Expressions and Identification Names Document.

**Prepackaged salted fish.** If the label uses a descriptive expression for salted fish, the corresponding salt or moisture content after salting must be met.

**Second container.** If labelled fish is placed in a second container resulting in prepackaged fish other than consumer prepackaged fish, the second container need not bear the net quantity declaration under paragraph 244.1(c).

### 266 — Imported Prepackaged Fish Origin Statement

The label of imported prepackaged fish must bear the name of the foreign state of origin.

### 268-270 — Variety Labelling for Fresh Apples; Imported Fresh Produce Origin Statements

**Prepackaged fresh apples.** The label must bear the variety name; this is not required on a second container if the apples were already properly labelled, unless consumer prepackaged.

**Definition of apple.** *Apple* means a fresh apple for which a grade is prescribed by these Regulations.

**Imported prepackaged fresh fruits or vegetables.** The principal display panel must bear "Product of"/"Produce of"/"produit de," "Grown in"/"cultivé dans," or "Country of Origin"/"pays d'origine," followed by the foreign state where grown, near the net quantity declaration or grade name — required whether or not subsequently repackaged in Canada, subject to a visibility exception for repackaging into a second non-consumer container.

**Type size.** This information must be in boldface characters at least 1.6 mm in height, except for consumer prepackaged fresh fruits or vegetables packaged from bulk at retail or sold as catch-weight foods.

### 272-274 — Fill Statements, Identification Names and Origin for Processed Fruit or Vegetable Products

**Prepackaged processed fruit or vegetable products.** The label must bear a slack-fill or short-weight statement if underfilled or below minimum drained weight standards, and "Keep Refrigerated"/"garder réfrigéré" for sauerkraut with preservative or non-hermetically-sealed fruit juice.

**Identification name — food in syrup or juice.** A food packaged frozen or hermetically sealed in syrup, fruit juice, or sweetened fruit juice must bear the identification name corresponding to its percentage of soluble solids.

**Name of foreign state.** An imported prepackaged processed fruit or vegetable product must bear the name of the foreign state where it was packaged, in characters at least 1.6 mm in height — or, if packaged for a Canadian importer under a private label, at least 6.4 mm (over 283.5 g) or 3.2 mm (283.5 g or less).

### 276-279 — Honey Origin Statements: Canadian, Imported and Blended

**Graded Canadian honey.** Prepackaged honey produced and graded in Canada must bear "Product of Canada"/"produit du Canada" or "Canadian Honey"/"miel canadien."

**Imported prepackaged honey.** Must bear "Product of"/"produit de" followed by the foreign state of origin, in characters at least 9.5 mm in height (for non-consumer prepackaged honey).

**Honey packaged from imported honey.** Consumer prepackaged honey packaged in Canada from imported, graded honey must bear "Product of"/"produit de" followed by the foreign state of origin.

**Blend of Canadian and imported honey.** Graded honey blends must bear an expression naming both Canadian and foreign origin(s), in descending order of proportion.

### 281 — Origin Statement for Imported Maple Products

The label of the following maple products must bear the name of the foreign state of origin: imported prepackaged maple syrup with a net quantity of 5 L or less, and any other imported prepackaged maple product with a net quantity of 5 kg or less.

### 282-283 — Inspection Legend and Label Requirements for Non-Prepackaged Edible Meat Products

**Inspection legend.** A non-prepackaged edible meat product must bear the Figure 1 Schedule 2 inspection legend (if sent interprovincially or exported) or the foreign state's official inspection mark (if imported); if applied directly to the product, the transverse axis through the legend's centre must be at least 25 mm long.

**Label requirements.** A label must be applied bearing the information required by paragraphs 218(1)(a)-(b) and 244.1(h) and sections 244.5 and 286 on the principal display panel, the ingredients in descending order by weight proportion, and ingredient components listed immediately after their ingredient, in descending proportion order, per sections B.01.009 and B.01.010 of the Food and Drug Regulations.

**Exception.** An ingredient need not be separately listed if all its components are shown by common name as if they were ingredients themselves.

### 284-285 — Omission, Substitution and Varying Proportions of Ingredients; Definitions

**Exception — omission or substitution.** Where it is acceptable manufacturing practice to omit or substitute an ingredient or component, the ingredient list may, for a 12-month period from label application, show the omittable/substitutable foods as ingredients if all such foods are listed, it is clearly stated they may be omitted or substituted, and they are grouped by food class in descending likely-use proportion.

**Exception — varying proportions.** Where proportions may vary, the same proportions may be shown throughout a 12-month period if it is clearly stated that proportions are subject to change and ingredients are listed in descending likely-use order.

**Definitions.** *Component* means an individual food unit combined with others to form an ingredient. *Ingredient* means an individual food unit combined with others to form an edible meat product.

### 286-287 — Refrigeration/Freezing Statement and Inspection Legend for Prepackaged Meat Products

**Refrigeration or freezing statement.** The principal display panel of a prepackaged edible meat product must bear a statement indicating it must be kept refrigerated or frozen, unless it is hermetically sealed and commercially sterile, dried to a water activity of 0.85 or less, has a pH of 4.6 or less, is packaged in salt or saturated salt solution, or is fermented with a final pH of 5.3 or less and water activity of 0.90 or less.

**Inspection legend.** The label must also bear the Figure 1 or 2 Schedule 2 inspection legend (if sent interprovincially or exported) or the foreign official inspection mark (if imported); for non-consumer prepackaged meat products this must appear on the principal display panel, or, if a tamper-resistant seal is used (other than on the bottom), it may appear on that seal instead.

### 289-291 — Animal Species, Ready-to-Eat Claims and Cooking Instructions

**Animal species.** A meat product label may describe it as derived from a carcass, part, cut, organ or tissue only if it bears the commonly known name of the animal species.

**Ready-to-eat and cooked claims.** A label may use "ready-to-eat" language only if section 47 requirements are met; "Cooked"/"Fully Cooked" may be used near the common name if the product was heat-treated long enough to produce the characteristics of a cooked meat product (friability, colour, texture, flavour).

**Uncooked meat products.** A prepackaged edible meat product that is not ready-to-eat but could be mistaken for one must bear, on the principal display panel near the common name, "Must Be Cooked"/"doit être cuit," "Raw Product"/"produit cru," "Uncooked"/"non cuit" or an equivalent, plus comprehensive cooking instructions (e.g., internal temperature and time) sufficient to render it ready-to-eat.

### 292-294 — Common Name and Packer Information for Poultry Carcasses

**Prepackaged poultry carcass.** For a graded, dressed or partially dressed poultry carcass, the common name must appear on the package over the breast (if individually packaged) or on a wishbone tag (if not).

**Seasoned consumer prepackaged poultry carcass.** A graded, seasoned, dressed/partially dressed carcass must bear "Seasoned"/"assaisonné."

**Poultry carcass — not individually packaged.** A graded carcass not individually packaged must have a wishbone tag bearing the name and principal place of business of the packager.

### 296 — Conditional Movement of Non-Compliant Labelled Meat Products Between Establishments

**Conditions for movement.** An edible meat product whose label does not meet these Regulations may be sent or conveyed from a licensed establishment if it is a tamper-resistant-sealed prepackaged product (other than consumer prepackaged) or is in a tamper-sealed conveyance, is sent to another establishment where meat products are processed by a licence holder, and is accompanied by a document attesting edibility under section 125 and a compliant ingredient list.

**Tamper-resistant seal.** The seal must not be broken until the meat product arrives at the receiving establishment.

### 297-298 — Imported Meat Product Origin Statement and Poultry Carcass Colour Matching

**Imported edible meat products.** The label must bear "Product of"/"produit de" followed by the foreign state of origin, near the common name, in the character height required by subsections 210(2) and (3) — applicable whether or not the product is subsequently packaged or labelled in Canada without further manufacture.

**Imported consumer prepackaged poultry carcasses.** For a graded, dressed or partially dressed imported carcass, the origin information must be shown in the same colour as the grade name.

### 299 — General Exceptions for Non-Retail Consumer Prepackaged Food

Paragraphs 199(1)(a) and 199(2)(a), subsections 199(3) to (5), and sections 200, 216, 221 to 224 and 229 to 241 do not apply to consumer prepackaged food that is manufactured for commercial/industrial/institutional use without retail sale, manufactured only for duty-free sale, or distributed without consideration.

### 304 — Definitions for Part 12 (Grades and Grade Names)

Key definitions for this Part include: *beef carcass*, *bison carcass*, *veal carcass* and *ovine carcass* (as defined in the Grades Document or Compendium); *grader* (a person designated under subsection 13(3) of the Canadian Food Inspection Agency Act); *grade stamp*, *roller brand*, *grade stamp applicator* and *grade roller* (tools and marks used in carcass grading); *grading stand* (platform for grading); *identification code* (traceability code applied before slaughter); *knife-rib* (the rib-cutting procedure exposing the longissimus muscles for evaluation); *primal cut* and *sub-primal cut* (carcass cut classifications); *marketing agency*; *meat inspection stamp*; *musculature*; *provincial establishment*; *trim*; *weighmaster*; and *yield class*/*yield stamp* (as defined in the Compendium or Grades Document).

### 305 — Prescribed Grade Names

For the purposes of the definition *grade name* in section 2 of the Act, the grade names set out in the Compendium, the Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Grades Document and the Grades Document are prescribed in respect of foods.

### 306 — Mandatory Grading for Interprovincial Trade, Import and Export

**Mandatory grading.** Eggs, fish, fresh fruits or vegetables, processed fruit or vegetable products, honey, maple syrup, or beef carcasses for which grades are prescribed, when sent, conveyed, imported or exported, must be graded, meet applicable grade requirements, and be labelled with the applicable grade name.

**Exception — subsection (1).** This does not apply to frozen gutted Pacific salmon; exported fresh fruits/vegetables; fresh fruits/vegetables sent/imported for manufacturing; certain specific fresh produce (blueberries, cantaloupes, crabapples, cranberries, field rhubarb, strawberries); certain hermetically sealed tomato and vegetable products; and imported or interprovincial ungraded beef carcasses/cuts labelled "Ungraded Beef"/"bœuf non classifié" or accompanied by supporting documents.

### 306(3)-(4) — Substandard Labelling and Foreign Grade Designation Exceptions

**Exception — substandard products.** Paragraphs (1)(b) and (c) do not apply to processed fruit/vegetable products or honey that fail grade requirements but are labelled "Substandard"/"sous-régulier" for interprovincial movement, or to honey exported with non-misleading quality information.

**Exception — foreign grade designations.** Paragraph (1)(c) does not apply to imported food labelled with a foreign grade designation (where the Compendium or Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Grades Document permits this), imported fresh fruits/vegetables sold in original containers meeting substantially equivalent foreign requirements, imported graded prepackaged fresh fruits/vegetables (other than consumer prepackaged) meeting Compendium/Grades Document requirements, and imported or interprovincial non-prepackaged beef carcasses/cuts graded under foreign requirements or accompanied by documents showing the applicable grade designation.

### 307-308 — Optional Grading and Authorized Application of Grade Names

**Optional grading.** Certain foods (those in paragraphs 306(2)(a),(c),(d); dairy products except exported ones; bison/ovine/veal carcasses; and dressed/partially dressed poultry carcasses), if graded and sent, conveyed, imported or exported, must meet and be labelled per the applicable grade requirements.

**Authorized application or use.** Subject to subsection (2), a licence holder may apply and use a grade name on a licensed food if it meets paragraphs 8(1)(a)-(d), meets the applicable grade requirements, complies with the Standards of Identity Document, was graded by a licence holder (for dairy, eggs, fish, processed fruit/vegetable products, honey, maple syrup) or by a grader (for dressed/partially dressed livestock or poultry carcasses), and is packaged and labelled in compliance with these Regulations.

**Livestock or poultry carcass.** Only persons referred to in the Compendium or Grades Document may apply or use a grade name on a dressed or partially dressed livestock or poultry carcass, under the circumstances set out there.

### 309-311 — Foreign Grade Designations, Authorized Reproduction and Advertising of Grade Names

**Imported foods — no prescribed grade name.** An imported food with no prescribed Canadian grade name may bear the foreign grade designation if it meets that designation's requirements, is otherwise compliant, and the foreign state's name is clearly shown.

**Authorized reproduction.** Label printers, package manufacturers, document publishers, and grade-stamp-applicator/grade-roller manufacturers are authorized to reproduce grade names under specified conditions tied to supplying authorized users.

**Advertising or sale.** Any person may use a grade name in advertising or selling a food if it is labelled with that grade name in compliance with these Regulations.

### 312-313 — Placement, Character Height and Illustration of Grade Names

**Labelling of grade name — consumer prepackaged food.** The grade name must appear on the principal display panel (or as set out in the Compendium or Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Grades Document) in the character height required by another provision, or by default per Schedule 6; for fresh fruits or vegetables, at least 1.6 mm.

**Illustration of grade name.** A grade name applied to a beef, bison, ovine or veal carcass, a dressed/partially dressed poultry carcass, a dairy product, or an egg must be shown as illustrated in the Compendium or Grades Document.

### 314-316 — Placement, Character Height and Size Designation for Egg Grade Names

**Grade name placement.** The grade name on prepackaged eggs must appear on top of a tray/carton, or centrally (excluding top/bottom) on other containers — with exceptions for eggs repackaged into a second container under specified visibility conditions.

**Type size.** Required minimum heights range from 1.5 mm to 13 mm depending on grade (Canada A/B vs. C/Nest Run) and container type.

**Eggs — Canada A.** Canada A eggs must bear the applicable size designation from the Compendium, shown near the grade name.

### 317-319 — Class, Size Designation and Type Size for Prepackaged Fish

**Class and size designation.** Prepackaged fish sent, conveyed, imported or exported must bear any applicable class and size designation from the Compendium, near the grade name, in characters at least 3.2 mm in height.

**Second container.** A second container need not repeat the grade name if the original labelled fish is repackaged as consumer prepackaged fish.

**Type size — small packages.** The grade name itself must be at least 3.2 mm in height if net quantity is 900 g or less.

### 320-321 — Grade Name Placement and Size Designation for Fresh Fruits or Vegetables

**Grade name — prepackaged fresh fruits or vegetables.** For non-consumer prepackaged fresh fruits or vegetables, the grade name must appear on any container surface except the bottom, in characters at least 1.6 mm in height; an exception applies for visible, legible labelling carried through to a second container.

**Size designation.** Interprovincially sent or imported fresh fruits or vegetables may bear the applicable size designation near the grade name, in characters at least 1.6 mm in height.

### 322 — Size Designation for Processed Fruit or Vegetable Products

A processed fruit or vegetable product sent, conveyed, imported or exported must bear any applicable size designation from the Compendium near the grade name, in characters at least 1.6 mm in height — except that certain hermetically sealed vegetables not size-graded or of mixed sizes may instead bear "Ungraded as to Size"/"non calibré" or "Assorted Sizes"/"Mixed Sizes" equivalents.

### 323-325 — Grade Name and Colour Class for Honey and Maple Syrup

**Grade name — prepackaged honey.** For non-consumer prepackaged honey, the grade name must appear on at least one side or end of the container, in characters at least 9.5 mm in height.

**Colour class — honey.** Honey sent, conveyed, imported or exported must bear the applicable colour class near the grade name, per Schedule 6 character heights (consumer prepackaged) or section 323 requirements (non-consumer prepackaged).

**Colour class — maple syrup.** Canada Grade A maple syrup sent/conveyed/exported, or Grade A imported maple syrup, must bear the applicable colour class in characters meeting the Schedule 6 minimum height.

### 326-329 — Grade Name Correspondence, Marking Removal and Additional Marks on Livestock Carcasses

**Prepackaged cut of beef.** A grade name on a prepackaged primal or sub-primal beef cut must correspond to the grade of the carcass it came from.

**Beef — Canada AAA.** An exported cut from a Canada AAA beef carcass in a container may instead be labelled "Canada Choice"/"Choix Canada."

**Removal of markings.** Grade stamps, roller brands or yield stamps must not be removed except under a grader's direction/supervision or when trimming for further processing; marked fat that is removed must be disposed of under supervision unless reapplied to the same carcass or, under supervision, to another carcass bearing the same mark (with Canada A/AA/AAA/Prime beef requiring reapplication to the rib).

**Additional marks.** A marked livestock carcass or primal cut may bear one additional mark (alone or with a date) not exceeding 76 mm in combined size and not touching the grade stamp, roller brand or yield stamp.

### 330-331 — Grade Name Placement, Type Size and Container Restrictions for Poultry Carcasses

**Placement.** The grade name must appear on the breast-covering part of the package (individually packaged) or on a wishbone tag (not individually packaged).

**Type size.** Minimum character heights range from 1.5 mm (unpackaged or "not for further processing" individually wrapped) up to 9 mm, depending on grade (Canada A/C vs. Utility) and net weight (thresholds at 1 kg and 5 kg).

**Packaging in same container.** Only graded, dressed or partially dressed poultry carcasses with the same common name may be packaged together.

### 332-333 — Conditions for Grading Eggs and Handling Ungraded or Rejected Eggs

**Conditions for grading.** A licence holder may grade an egg only if it is edible, has no abnormal odour, is not mouldy, has not been incubated, has no internal defect, and is of usual colour — except that an egg with an oviduct particle or blood spot no larger than 3 mm in diameter may still be graded Canada C.

**Ungraded eggs.** Ungraded eggs received at a grading establishment must be graded and labelled with the applicable grade name, or rejected if they fail to meet grade requirements.

**Rejected eggs.** Rejected eggs must be destroyed or placed in a container labelled "Rejects"/"rejetés."

### 334-335 — Request for and Conditions of Livestock Carcass Grading

**Request for grading.** A grader may grade a livestock carcass in a licensed or provincial establishment upon written request from an establishment authority, a producer, or the person in possession of the carcass.

**Conditions for grading.** Grading requires that the carcass bear a meat inspection stamp (or foreign mark for imported beef); occur in the appropriate slaughter/processing establishment; be weighed by an approved-scale weighmaster; be presented at an adequately lit grading stand or cooler; undergo a knife-rib (beef/bison) or brisket cut (veal) at least 10 minutes beforehand; and that the establishment have adequate equipment/facilities and properly functioning, accurate grading equipment.

### 336-337 — Grading Stand Requirements and Pre-Weighing Rule

**Adequate facilities.** More than 400 carcasses graded per hour requires more than one grading stand; stands must be height-adjustable with minimum dimensions scaling from 3m×2m (≤150/hr) to 5m×2m (>300/hr).

**Weighing before trimming.** A graded livestock carcass must be weighed before trimming, unless an inspector or grader directs otherwise.

### 338 — Conditions for Grading Dressed and Partially Dressed Poultry Carcasses

**Dressed carcass.** Grading requires the carcass to come from poultry slaughtered in a licensed/provincial establishment, to have been inspected under the Act or equivalent provincial law, to not be dried out (if chilled) or discoloured from insufficient bleeding, to contain no more than one heart/liver/gizzard/neck, to have an intact breast bone (if over 900 g), and to not have been basted or stuffed.

**Partially dressed carcass.** Additionally requires evisceration, removal of epidermis from feet/shanks and of claws, a wrapped head (if present), and a clean beak (if present).

**Grading location.** Poultry carcasses must be graded only in a licensed or provincial establishment.

### 339 — Conditions for Issuance and Contents of Grading Certificates

**Conditions for issuance.** A grading certificate for a livestock carcass or lot may be issued if the producer requested it in writing at delivery, identified each animal with an identification code, and filed a list linking codes to the producer — and if the identification code was retained or transferred to the carcass after slaughter.

**Contents of certificate.** The certificate, signed by the grader, must include producer details, establishment details, certificate number, slaughter date, and per-carcass identification code, warm weight and grade; for lots, the count per grade/yield class and number condemned; for Canada A/AA/AAA/Prime beef, the yield; and for beef/bison carcasses, additional detail on age, meat colour, firmness, musculature, marbling, fat colour/texture, fat measurement and pronounced masculinity (with corresponding lamb and mutton carcass detail requirements).

**Recording of information.** This information may be recorded on the certificate by the licence holder, provincial establishment operator, or marketing agency under the grader's direction.

### 340 — Definitions for Part 13 (Organic Products)

Key definitions include: *aquaculture product* (as defined in CAN/CGSB-32.312); *CAN/CGSB-32.310*, *CAN/CGSB-32.311* and *CAN/CGSB-32.312* (Canadian General Standards Board organic production standards); *certification body* (accredited under section 361 or 363); *conformity verification body* (entity under agreement with the Agency to assess/accredit certification bodies per ISO/IEC 17011); *ISO/IEC 17011* and *ISO/IEC 17065* (international conformity assessment standards).

### 341 — Prescribed Food Commodities and Feed Definition for Organic Products

**Definition of food commodity.** For paragraph (c) of the Act's definition, *feed* (under the Feeds Act) and *seed* (under the Seeds Act) are prescribed food commodities.

**Feed.** For this purpose, "livestock" in the Feeds Act definition of *feed* includes livestock that is an aquaculture product.

**Exemption.** A food commodity not included in paragraph (a) or (b) of the Act's *food commodity* definition is exempt from provisions not necessary to give effect to this Part — except section 6 of the Act, which is never exempted.

### 342-343 — Packaging/Labelling Restriction and Organic Percentage Calculation

**Packaging and labelling.** The packaging and labelling of an organic product to be sent or conveyed interprovincially may only be conducted by a person holding a certificate granted under section 345 or 348.

**Determination of percentage of organic products.** The percentage of organic content in a multi-ingredient food commodity must be determined in accordance with CAN/CGSB-32.310.

### 344 — Application for Organic Certification of a Food Commodity

**Application.** Any person wishing to obtain organic certification of a food commodity must apply in writing to a certification body.

**Contents of application.** The application must include the food commodity's name; a statement of substances/materials used and how; a detailed methods document and control mechanisms ensuring compliance with CAN/CGSB-32.312 (aquaculture) or CAN/CGSB-32.310 (other); details of any third-party packager/labeller and their certificate; and, for multi-ingredient commodities, composition and organic content percentage.

**Time of application.** Applications must be filed within 12 months before expected sale, or at least 15 months before for maple products, field/greenhouse soil-grown crops, wild crops, and aquaculture products with production cycles over 12 months.

### 345 — Certification of a Food Commodity as Organic

**Certification.** A certification body must conduct an on-site verification and certify a food commodity as organic if the substances/materials and methods used meet the applicable CAN/CGSB standards, any third-party packager/labeller holds a valid certificate, multi-ingredient commodities are at least 70% organic content and meet CAN/CGSB-32.310 composition requirements, and the application information is complete, truthful and not misleading.

**Certificate.** The certification body must provide a certificate confirming organic certification, naming the food commodity, the applicable standard (CAN/CGSB-32.310 or -32.312), and, for multi-ingredient commodities, whether at least 70% or at least 95% organic content.

### 346 — Periodic Information Updates and On-Site Verification for Organic Certification

**Requirement to provide information.** The certificate holder must provide the section 344(2) information to the certification body every 12 months from issuance, no later than six months before the period ends.

**On-site verification.** After receiving that information, the certification body must conduct an on-site verification, no later than the end of the 12-month period, to confirm continued compliance with subsection 345(1).

### 347-348 — Application for and Certification of Organic Packaging or Labelling

**Application for certification.** A person wishing to package or label an organic product (other than one they already hold a section 345 certificate for) must apply in writing to a certification body, including the product type, substances/materials and methods to be used, and control mechanisms ensuring compliance with the applicable CAN/CGSB standard.

**Certification.** The certification body must conduct an on-site verification and certify the packaging/labelling activity if the substances, materials and methods meet the applicable standard's requirements and general principles.

**Certificate and validity.** The certification body must provide a certificate indicating the organic product type and the validity period, which is 12 months from the date granted.

### 349 — Suspension of Organic Certification

**Grounds for suspension.** Subject to subsection (2), a certification body must suspend a certification if the holder fails to comply with the Act or this Part; uses non-compliant substances/materials; allows contact with non-compliant substances/materials; uses compliant substances but not as prescribed; uses methods that fail to meet organic production requirements; or, for multi-ingredient commodities, falls below 70% organic content.

**Conditions, extension and notice.** Suspension requires a prior written report with grounds and a corrective-action period, and failure to act within that period (or any single extension granted on written request); the certification body must notify the holder in writing of the suspension and its effective date.

**Duration.** The suspension must be lifted once the certification body determines corrective action has been taken.

### 350 — Cancellation of Organic Certification

**Grounds for cancellation.** A certification body must cancel a certification if the holder fails to take corrective action within 30 days of suspension; was non-compliant with section 15 of the Act at application or during validity; or, while suspended, continues prohibited interprovincial movement, labelling, advertising or use of the organic product legend, or continues to package/label an organic product (for section 348 certifications).

**Conditions, notice.** Cancellation requires prior written notice of grounds and an opportunity to be heard; the certification body must then notify the holder in writing of the cancellation and its effective date.

### 351-352 — Document Keeping and Notification of Changes Affecting Organic Certification

**Documents.** The certificate holder must prepare, keep and maintain documents as required by CAN/CGSB-32.312 (aquaculture) or CAN/CGSB-32.310 (other commodities).

**Changes affecting certification.** The holder must immediately notify the certification body in writing of any change that could affect certification, and of any complaint received regarding the organic integrity of the certified product.

### 353 — Use of "Organic" and Related Expressions on Labels and in Advertising

**General rule.** Expressions like "organic"/"biologique"/"organique," "organically grown," "organically raised," "organically produced" (and similar/abbreviated/phonetic forms) may be used on the label or in advertising of a food commodity sent or conveyed interprovincially only if it is an organic product and, for multi-ingredient commodities, at least 95% organic content.

**Expression "organic ingredients."** A multi-ingredient organic product with less than 95% organic content may instead use "organic ingredients"/"d'ingrédients biologiques," immediately preceded by the rounded-down percentage, shown in matching character height and prominence.

**Non-organic multi-ingredient commodities.** The ingredient list of a multi-ingredient food commodity that is not itself an organic product may still indicate which individual ingredients are organic.

### 354-355 — Additional Required Information and Official Languages for Organic Labelling

**Additional information.** Where an organic expression appears on a label, it must also bear the certifying body's name (or, for imports, the foreign-accredited entity's name); for multi-ingredient commodities, identification of organic contents in the ingredient list; and, for imports bearing the Schedule 9 product legend, "Product of"/"produit de" plus the origin country or "Imported"/"importé" near the legend.

**Official languages.** These expressions and information must generally appear in both official languages, except for feed, seed, or foods where the Food and Drug Regulations permit single-language labelling.

### 356-357 — Interprovincial Trade and Import Requirements for Organic-Labelled Food Commodities

**Interprovincial trade.** A food commodity sent or conveyed interprovincially and labelled/advertised with an organic expression must be an organic product, meet the relevant expression's requirements, and meet sections 354-355; non-organic multi-ingredient commodities may still flag organic ingredients in the list.

**Import.** An imported food commodity labelled/advertised with an organic expression must be certified organic under subsection 345(1), or certified by an entity accredited by a foreign state with a recognized agreement/arrangement with the Agency (or by extension through such an entity), and must meet the relevant expression's requirements and sections 354-355.

**Demonstration and retention.** The importer must be able to demonstrate compliance by providing an organic certification certificate on request from the Minister or an inspector, retained for five years after import.

### 358-359 — Organic Product Legend: Definition and Authorized Use

**Definition.** The product legend set out in Schedule 9 is the prescribed *inspection mark* for organic products under section 2 of the Act.

**Application or use.** Any person may apply and use the Schedule 9 legend on a food commodity that is an organic product (and, for multi-ingredient commodities, at least 95% organic content).

**Advertisement, sale and non-food items.** The same threshold applies to advertising/selling a product bearing the legend; the legend may also be applied for advertising or informational purposes to any non-food item to which the Act applies.

### 360-361 — Application for and Granting of Certification Body Accreditation

**Application for accreditation.** A person wishing to be accredited as a certification body must apply in writing to a conformity verification body and undergo an ISO/IEC 17011 assessment verifying compliance with ISO/IEC 17065, organic certification knowledge, and the validity of their certification methodology and results.

**Accreditation.** On a conformity verification body's recommendation with supporting documents, the Minister must accredit the applicant, provide an accreditation number, and notify them in writing of the five-year validity period.

### 362 — Refusal to Recommend Accreditation

If the conformity verification body refuses to recommend the applicant's accreditation, it must send them a written notice by registered mail that states the reasons for the decision and notifies the applicant of their right to make a request, within 30 days after the day on which they receive the notice, to the Minister for a review of the decision. The conformity verification body must also send a copy of the notice to the Minister.

### 363 — Ministerial Review of a Refusal to Recommend Accreditation

The Minister must, at the written request of the applicant, review the decision referred to in section 362.

If the Minister decides to confirm the conformity verification body's decision, the Minister must provide a copy of his or her decision with reasons to the applicant.

If the Minister does not confirm the decision, the Minister must accredit the applicant, provide them with an accreditation number, and notify them in writing of the period of validity referred to in subsection 361(2).

## Division 8 — Conformity Verification Bodies and Certification Bodies
 
### Suspension
 
Subject to the conditions in subsection 364(2), on the recommendation of a conformity verification body, the Minister must suspend the accreditation of a certification body if it does not comply with any provision of the Act, this Part, or ISO/IEC 17065.
 
### Conditions (Section 364(2))
 
The Minister must not suspend an accreditation unless the certification body:
 
(a) was provided with a written report that sets out the grounds for the suspension and the period within which corrective action must be taken in order to avoid the suspension; and
 
(b) failed to take corrective action within that period or, if the conformity verification body granted an extension at the written request of the certification body, within any later period specified by the conformity verification body.
 
### Extension of Period (Section 364(3))
 
The conformity verification body may grant an extension of the period in which corrective action must be taken only once.
 
### Written Notice of Suspension (Section 364(4))
 
The Minister must notify the certification body in writing of the suspension and the date on which it takes effect.
 
### Provision of Lists (Section 364(5))
 
The certification body must provide the Minister, within 15 days after the day on which the suspension takes effect, with a list of the holders of the certificates that it has granted and a list of pending applications for certification.
 
### Duration of Suspension (Section 364(6))
 
The suspension of an accreditation must be lifted if the Minister determines, on the recommendation of the conformity verification body, that corrective action has been taken.
 
### Cancellation (Section 365(1))
 
On the recommendation of a conformity verification body, the Minister must cancel an accreditation if the certification body:
 
(a) fails to take corrective action within 30 days after the day on which the accreditation was suspended;
 
(b) was not in compliance with section 15 of the Act in respect of the application made under section 360 or at any time during the period of validity of the accreditation; or
 
(c) continues, while their accreditation is suspended, to accept applications for certification, to make determinations under subsection 345(1) or 348(1), to suspend certifications under subsection 349(1), or to cancel certifications under subsection 350(1).
 
### Conditions for Cancellation (Section 365(2))
 
The Minister must not cancel an accreditation unless the certification body was notified in writing of the grounds for the cancellation and was provided with an opportunity to be heard in respect of the cancellation.
 
### Written Notice of Cancellation (Section 365(3))
 
The Minister must notify the certification body in writing of the cancellation and the date on which it takes effect.
 
## Part 14 — Seizure and Detention
 
### Detention Tag (Section 366)
 
An inspector who seizes and detains a thing under section 25 of the Act must apply or attach a detention tag to it which bears the following:
 
(a) the expression "UNDER DETENTION" and the word "RETENU", in capital letters;
 
(b) the detention tag number;
 
(c) a description of the thing;
 
(d) the reason for the seizure and detention;
 
(e) the date of the seizure and detention; and
 
(f) the inspector's name and signature.
 
### Prohibition — Removal of Detention Tag (Section 367)
 
It is prohibited for a person to remove a detention tag from a thing that has been seized and detained unless authorized to do so by an inspector.
 
### Notice of Detention (Section 368(1))
 
As soon as feasible after a thing has been seized and detained, an inspector must provide a notice of detention to its owner or to the person having possession, care, or control of it at the time of its seizure.
 
### Content of Notice of Detention (Section 368(2))
 
The notice of detention must indicate that the thing was seized and detained under section 25 of the Act and set out:
 
(a) the detention tag number;
 
(b) a description of the thing;
 
(c) the reason for the seizure and detention;
 
(d) the date of the seizure and detention;
 
(e) the place of the seizure and detention;
 
(f) the inspector's name and signature; and
 
(g) a telephone number to call for further information about the seizure and detention.
 
### Storage Conditions (Section 369)
 
Anything that is seized must be stored by the person to whom the notice of detention is provided, under storage conditions that are appropriate for its preservation and at the person's expense.
 
### Notice of Release (Section 370)
 
If a thing is released under section 30 of the Act, an inspector must provide a notice of release to the person to whom the notice of detention was provided.

### 18-Month Delay (Section 371(1))
 
Subsections 5(2) and 7(2), section 11, paragraphs 15(1)(a) and (b), subsections 18(3) and 19(2), and Parts 4 and 5 do not apply, for the 18-month period that begins on the day on which these Regulations come into force, in respect of foods other than dairy products, eggs, processed egg products, fish, fresh fruits or vegetables, processed fruit or vegetable products, honey, maple products, and meat products.

Section 11 and Part 4 do not apply to any person who did not have more than four employees at any one time during the last 12 months of the 18-month period referred to in subsection 371(1), in respect of foods other than dairy products, eggs, processed egg products, fish, fresh fruits or vegetables, processed fruit or vegetable products, honey, maple products, and meat products, for 12 months after the last day of that period.
 
### Additional Delay — $100,000 or Less (Section 371(3))
 
Sections 11 and 45 to 85 do not apply to any person whose gross sales derived from food were $100,000 or less for the last 12 months of the 18-month period referred to in subsection 371(1), in respect of foods other than dairy products, eggs, processed egg products, fish, fresh fruits or vegetables, processed fruit or vegetable products, honey, maple products, and meat products, for 12 months after the last day of that period.

For the 12-month period that begins on the day on which these Regulations come into force, section 11 and Part 4 do not apply in respect of fresh fruits or vegetables, and Part 5 does not apply in respect of fresh fruits or vegetables, to any person who grows or harvests fresh fruits or vegetables unless the person is the holder of a licence to conduct an activity in respect of those fresh fruits or vegetables.

Part 13 (Organic Products) does not apply in respect of aquaculture products — other than seaweed in respect of which a certification has been issued under section 13 of the Organic Products Regulations, 2009 — for the 24-month period that begins on the day on which these Regulations come into force.

A food commodity that, immediately before the day on which these Regulations come into force, meets the requirements that apply to it under the Consumer Packaging and Labelling Act, the Fish Inspection Act, the Food and Drugs Act, the Meat Inspection Act, and the Canada Agricultural Products Act is deemed, as of that coming-into-force day, to meet the requirements that apply under these Regulations with respect to the manufacturing, preparing, storing, packaging, and labelling of that food commodity, if those activities are conducted before that day.
 
### Reference to Act (Section 374(2))
 
In paragraph 29(1)(a), subparagraph 30(a)(ii), and paragraph 39(c), a reference to "the Act" must be read to include a reference to any provisions of the Consumer Packaging and Labelling Act that apply in respect of a food, the Fish Inspection Act, the Meat Inspection Act, and the Canada Agricultural Products Act, as they read immediately before the coming into force of these Regulations.

## Schedule 5 — Sampling Methodology for Net Quantity
 
### Part 1 — Minimum Sample Size
 
Schedule 5 (referenced in subsection 200(2) and paragraphs 200(4)(a) and (b)) sets out the methodology for net quantity sampling. Part 1 specifies the minimum number of units required in a sample, based on the number of units in the lot:
 
| Number of Units in the Lot | Minimum Number of Units in the Sample |
|---|---|
| ≥ 2 but ≤ 10 | All units in the lot |
| ≥ 11 but ≤ 128 | 25% of the units in the lot, rounded up, but not less than 10 |
| ≥ 129 but ≤ 4,000 | 32 |
| ≥ 4,001 but ≤ 8,000 | 64 |
| ≥ 8,001 but ≤ 12,000 | 96 |
| > 12,000 | 125 |
 
### Part 2 — Formula for Weighted Average Quantity
 
For the purposes of paragraph 200(4)(a), Part 2 of Schedule 5 sets out the formula for adjusting the sample mean to determine the weighted average quantity of the units in a sample:
 
**Xa = x̄ + s(t ÷ √n)**
 
Where:
- **Xa** is the weighted average quantity of the units in the sample.
- **x̄** (the sample mean) is calculated as ∑x ÷ n, where ∑x is the sum of the net quantities of all units in the sample and n is the number of units in the sample.
- **s** (the standard deviation of the sample) is calculated as √(∑(x − x̄)² ÷ (n − 1)), where ∑(x − x̄)² is the sum of the squared differences between the sample mean and the net quantity of each unit.
- **t** is the value determined in accordance with Part 3 of this Schedule for the selected sample size.
Note: the value of (t ÷ √n) may, instead of being calculated directly, be determined using the applicable value set out in column 3 of the table in Part 3.
 
### Part 3 — Table of t and (t ÷ √n) Values
 
Part 3 of Schedule 5 provides a statistical lookup table giving the value of **t** and **(t ÷ √n)** for sample sizes from 2 to 125. Selected values include:
 
| Sample Size | t | (t ÷ √n) |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | 63.657 | 45.01 |
| 5 | 4.604 | 2.06 |
| 10 | 3.250 | 1.03 |
| 20 | 2.861 | 0.640 |
| 30 | 2.756 | 0.503 |
| 32 | 2.746 | 0.485 |
| 64 | 2.657 | 0.332 |
| 96 | 2.634 | 0.269 |
| 125 | 2.615 | 0.234 |
 
If all units in a lot are selected to constitute the sample, zero must be used as the value of both t and (t ÷ √n). For sample sizes greater than 32 but less than 125 (other than 64 or 96), the value of t is determined by linear interpolation using the formula:
 
**t = a − ((a − b) × (c − e) ÷ (c − d))**
 
where *a* is the t-value for the closest sample size below the selected size, *b* is the t-value for the closest sample size above, *c* is 120 divided by the closest sample size below, *d* is 120 divided by the closest sample size above, and *e* is 120 divided by the selected sample size. In such cases, the value of (t ÷ √n) is calculated in accordance with Part 2.

## Schedule 9 — Product Legend
 
### Organic Product Legend Description
 
Schedule 9 (referenced in clauses 350(1)(c)(i)(B) and (D), paragraph 354(d), and sections 358 and 359) describes the official "Canada Organic / Biologique Canada" product legend, which depicts a circular emblem with a maple leaf over stylized hills.
 
The legend may appear in black with a white background, in black with a transparent background, or in colour. If rendered in colour: the background is white or transparent; the outer and inner borders and the hills are green (Pantone no. 368); the maple leaf is red (Pantone no. 186); and the lettering is black.
